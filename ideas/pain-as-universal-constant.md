# Pain as Universal Constant

The central psychological claim of *Everything Is F*cked*: pain, not happiness, is the universal constant of human experience. Our perception and expectations warp themselves to fit a predetermined amount of pain, regardless of external conditions. No matter how sunny our skies, our mind will always imagine just enough clouds to be slightly disappointed.

This is why happiness researchers consistently find that people hover around a "seven" on a 1-10 scale regardless of circumstances. Lottery winners return to baseline happiness within months. People paralyzed in accidents also return to baseline. The hedonic treadmill ensures that "more" never reaches "enough."

The implication is radical: **the pursuit of happiness is self-defeating.** If pain is constant and perception adjusts, chasing pleasure just recalibrates the baseline. The only coherent response is to choose *which* pain you endure -- to select pain that produces growth rather than pain you merely numb.

This maps directly onto Taleb's concept of antifragility. Systems that avoid all stress become fragile. Systems that engage with appropriate stress become stronger. The human mind works the same way: avoiding pain shrinks your world and increases your sensitivity to ever-smaller threats (the Blue Dot Effect). Engaging with pain expands your capacity and reduces your fragility.

The ancient philosophers understood this. Plato, Aristotle, and the Stoics spoke not of happiness but of character -- the ability to sustain pain and make appropriate sacrifices. The Enlightenment mistook the *improvement* of pain for the *elimination* of pain, and invented the disastrous idea of "the pursuit of happiness" as a cultural value.

The question is never "How do I avoid pain?" but "What pain is worth suffering?" The quality of your life is determined not by the absence of pain but by the quality of your relationship to it.
