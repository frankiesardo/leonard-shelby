# The Feedback Loop from Hell

You feel anxious. Then you feel anxious *about* feeling anxious. Then you feel guilty that you can't stop being anxious about being anxious. Each layer of meta-emotion compounds the original suffering. The same loop works with anger (angry at yourself for getting angry), sadness (depressed about being depressed), and guilt (guilty for feeling guilty).

Social media pours gasoline on this: you feel bad, you open your phone, you see 350 people having amazing lives, you feel worse about feeling bad, and now you're deeper in the loop than when you started.

The antidote is aggressively simple: not giving a fuck that you feel bad. "I feel like shit, but who gives a fuck?" This short-circuits the loop by refusing to add the meta-layer. The original feeling is still there, but it's no longer compounding.

## Why it matters

This is one of the main mechanisms by which normal discomfort becomes chronic suffering. The first-order emotion (anxiety, anger, sadness) is often survivable and transient. It's the second-order emotion (anxiety *about* anxiety) that turns it into a spiral. Manson argues that modern culture — with its insistence that you should feel happy all the time — has made the Feedback Loop from Hell a borderline epidemic. Back when Grandpa felt like shit, he thought "that's just life" and went back to shoveling hay. Now, if you feel bad for five minutes, you assume something is wrong with you.

The loop also explains why much conventional self-help backfires: telling someone they should feel positive can make them feel guilty for not feeling positive, which makes them feel worse.
