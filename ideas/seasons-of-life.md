"Life and business is like the changing seasons." Jim Rohn's four-part metaphor captures the cyclical nature of all human experience. The key phrase: *you cannot change the seasons, but you can change yourself*. That's how life gets better — not by chance, but by change.

Four lessons:

1. **Learn to handle the winters.** They come right after fall — with regularity. Economic winters, personal winters, when your heart is smashed and the nights are unusually long. You can't get rid of January by tearing it off the calendar. But you can get stronger, wiser, and better. "Don't wish it was easier — wish you were better."

2. **Learn to take advantage of the spring.** Opportunity follows difficulty. But you must *take advantage* — quickly, because springs are few. "You have to get good at one of two things: planting in the spring or begging in the fall."

3. **Learn to protect your crops all summer.** All good will be attacked. Every garden will be invaded. All values must be defended. This isn't pessimism — it's realism about the constant war between enterprise and ease.

4. **Learn to reap in the fall without complaint.** Take full responsibility. No apology if you do well. No complaint if you don't. Go to the mirror: that's where you find who planted your crop.

This maps directly to [stress and recovery](stress-and-recovery.md) — winter is stress, spring is recovery, and the cycle repeats. It's also [making sandals](making-sandals.md) at the macro level: you adapt yourself to the terrain rather than cursing the rocks.
