# Courage Over Genius

"I found a lot more high IQ in industry than courage. I found a lot more maybe even genius than courage." Courage — to disagree, to create, to start — is rarer and more load-bearing than intelligence.

Tobi Lütke's observation: when someone disagrees with him, he immediately stops to find the point of divergence in foundational assumptions, because the disagreer might be right. But most people don't disagree. Not because they can't think, but because they lack the courage to voice it. The same bottleneck applies to entrepreneurship, creative work, and any form of putting yourself out there.

The implication for leaders: instead of demanding more courage from people, lower the threshold. Instagram's "trial run" feature — posting a reel to strangers before your followers see it — is one of the most profound pieces of software Tobi has encountered, because it removes fear from creation. Every system that makes it easier to try and cheaper to fail is expanding human potential.
