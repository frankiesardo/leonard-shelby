Jim Rohn names four emotions that can change your entire life in a single day. They are the raw material of transformation — the feeling precedes the action, and the action rewires the life.

**Disgust.** The most underrated catalyst. "I've had it." The man who's finally had it with mediocrity, with being a loser, with the sick feeling of watching his wife buy the 37-cent can of beans to save two cents. Rohn's own turning point was lying to a Girl Scout about not having two dollars for cookies. He closed the door and said: *I don't want to live like this anymore.* Disgust doesn't end it that day — but it *begins* it.

**Decision.** Inner civil war resolved. "If it's easy, do it easy. If it's hard, do it hard. Just get it done." Many times, after you've decided, getting on with it is easier than deciding. Decision is often the toughest part.

**Desire.** It comes from inside, not outside. You don't send off for it. But it can be *triggered* — by a book, a song, a conversation, an event. "Welcome every human experience. You never know which one is going to turn it all on." This is why you [get on the plane](get-on-the-plane.md).

**Resolve.** "Promising yourself you will never give up." The most powerful of the four. "I'll do it or die." The world steps aside for that kind of will. A baby doesn't set a deadline on learning to walk — it keeps trying until it walks. That's the formula.

**Action** is what finally makes all four emotions real. "Don't just be listeners — be doers." Without action, even the most intense emotion dissipates. This connects to [investment in loss](investment-in-loss.md): the willingness to act means accepting you'll sometimes fail, and that's part of the process.
