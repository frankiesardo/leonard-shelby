# Rational and Anti-Rational Memes

Memes -- ideas that replicate from person to person -- come in two fundamentally different kinds, distinguished by their replication strategy.

**Anti-rational memes** replicate by disabling their holders' critical faculties. They survive not because they are true or useful, but because they prevent people from questioning them. Religions that instill fear of supernatural punishment. Gender norms that make deviation feel shameful. Taboos that make certain questions unthinkable. These memes contain sophisticated implicit knowledge about human psychological vulnerabilities, and they exploit that knowledge ruthlessly. They do not need to be true; in fact, falsehood often helps, because a false threat (an unfalsifiable hobgoblin) is unbounded and therefore more terrifying than any real danger.

**Rational memes** replicate by *relying on* their holders' critical faculties. They survive because they are true, useful, beautiful, or otherwise valuable -- and because, under criticism, they outcompete their rivals. Newton's laws spread not through fear or conformity but because they worked for cathedrals and bridges and artillery alike. Their fidelity depends on the tradition of criticism: in the absence of critical scrutiny, true ideas lose their replication advantage and can be crowded out by anti-rational competitors.

The two strategies are mutually exclusive. A meme that is partly rational and partly anti-rational inherits the weaknesses of both and the strengths of neither.

## Why it matters

This distinction explains why the Enlightenment is fragile and incomplete. In domains where rational memes dominate -- the physical sciences, certain political and economic institutions -- progress is rapid and sustained. In domains still controlled by anti-rational memes -- much of education, aesthetic convention, many social norms -- people enact elaborate rituals while believing they are making free choices.

The test for an anti-rational meme is Deutsch's diagnostic: whenever you find yourself enacting complex, narrowly defined behavior that has been accurately repeated across many holders, and especially when your own explanations for that behavior are bad explanations, you should be suspicious. "Because I say so" and "it never did me any harm" are the signatures of anti-rational memes.

The Enlightenment is the ongoing project of replacing anti-rational memes with rational ones. It is nowhere near complete.
