# Universal Explainers

People are universal explainers. The human brain can, in principle, understand anything that is understandable. There is no law of physics that makes a phenomenon comprehensible to one type of mind but not to another, because the laws of physics are themselves understandable, and understanding them gives access to everything they govern.

This is a consequence of the universality of computation (Turing's discovery) and the universality of explanation (Deutsch's extension). A human brain is a universal computer in the relevant sense: given enough time and resources, it can perform any computation that any other computer can perform. More importantly, it can *create explanatory knowledge* -- the only process known to produce genuinely new understanding. Biological evolution also creates knowledge, but it is bounded and parochial: a species can adapt to its environment, but it cannot understand its environment or deliberately transform it.

Deutsch argues that this universality gives people a role of cosmic significance. A galaxy is vastly larger than a person, but it contains no explanatory knowledge and cannot act on what it does not understand. A person can understand the galaxy, predict its behavior, and in principle transform it. The future of the physical universe -- what transformations will occur, what will be created, what problems will be solved -- depends on what people choose to do.

## Why it matters

The implication is that there can be no "superhuman" intelligence in the way science fiction imagines it. Since humans are already universal explainers, future AI -- if it is achieved -- will be an equal, not a superior. It will be able to think faster, with more working memory, but it will not be able to understand anything that a human brain cannot, in principle, also understand. The difference is one of speed and resources, not of fundamental capability.

This also means that the Singularity, as popularly conceived -- the moment when machine intelligence surpasses human comprehension -- is a misconception. What lies ahead is not transcendence but collaboration: universal explainers (biological and artificial) working together to create knowledge and solve problems at increasing speed.
