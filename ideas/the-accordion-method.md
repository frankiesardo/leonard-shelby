# The Accordion Method

Prepare talks by speaking, not writing. Start at three minutes. Compress to two, then one, then thirty seconds — each round forces you to shed noise and find the essential pillars. Then expand back up: thirty seconds to one minute to two to three. By the time you open back up, you have a football field of space and only the strongest ideas remain.

The result is a talk that's internalized (not memorized), plastic (works at any time length), and impossible to derail because you know the structure in your bones. There's no script to forget. You've discovered the pillars through progressive compression, and those pillars hold up regardless of the time you're given.
