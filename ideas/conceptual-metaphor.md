# Conceptual Metaphor

Metaphor is not a decoration layered on top of literal language. It is the fundamental structure of human thought. When we say "I *demolished* his argument" or "we need to *budget* our time," we are not using figures of speech -- we are revealing the conceptual mappings through which we actually understand argument (as war) and time (as money). The linguistic expressions are evidence of the metaphor, not the metaphor itself.

Lakoff and Johnson identify three main types. **Orientational metaphors** map spatial experience onto abstract domains: HAPPY IS UP, SAD IS DOWN, MORE IS UP, LESS IS DOWN. These are grounded directly in bodily experience -- drooping posture goes with sadness, adding substance raises a pile. **Ontological metaphors** let us treat abstract experiences as entities and substances: inflation as a thing we can "combat," the mind as a machine that "runs" or "breaks down." **Structural metaphors** map the full structure of one experiential domain onto another: ARGUMENT IS WAR, TIME IS MONEY, LIFE IS A JOURNEY.

The key insight is systematicity. ARGUMENT IS WAR is not one isolated expression -- it generates an entire coherent system: we *attack* positions, *defend* claims, use *strategies*, *win* and *lose*, find positions *indefensible*. The metaphor is productive: new expressions that fit the mapping are immediately understood. And the metaphor shapes behavior: we *conduct* arguments combatively because we *conceive* of them combatively. A culture that understood ARGUMENT IS DANCE would argue -- and live -- very differently.

## Why it matters

If thought is metaphorical, then changing the metaphors people use changes how they think and act. This is why political rhetoric works: Carter's framing of the energy crisis as war licensed military-style responses, while Lovins's SOFT vs. HARD PATHS metaphor would have led to completely different policy. It is why product naming matters, why framing effects dominate negotiations, and why the language you use to describe your own life shapes the life you actually live.

It also means there is no "literal" baseline of thought that exists free of metaphor. Even scientific concepts are built on metaphorical foundations -- "high-energy particles" (MORE IS UP), "information flow" (the Conduit metaphor), "natural selection" (nature as an agent that selects). The metaphors are invisible precisely because they are so deeply embedded.
