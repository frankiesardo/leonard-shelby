# The Bow and Arrow

Shift from "what do I want to say" to "what do I want my audience to remember." The answer should be a single sentence — the arrow. Everything else (stories, data, anecdotes) is the bow that gives the arrow weight and impact.

Works at macro (whole talk) and micro (each slide, each section) levels. If a slide doesn't have a single thing you want people to remember, it shouldn't exist or should be split. The framework forces clarity: you can't deploy the bow and arrow until you know what your arrow is, and most speakers discover they don't actually know.
