# Software Has a Worldview

Every piece of software you adopt carries the worldview of its creators. Use Workday, and you adopt Workday's vision of how HR should work. Use Salesforce, and you adopt their assumptions about the sales process. The constraints are silent: "We've all been in meetings where it's, 'Oh, we can't do that because the system doesn't support it.'"

This is why Shopify builds so much internally — not out of Not-Invented-Here syndrome, but because if your ideas about compensation, project management, or product review differ from what's commercially available, off-the-shelf software silently constrains your decisions. Building your own tools is expensive, but adopting someone else's worldview is more expensive if it's the wrong one.

The implication extends to Shopify's own product: Shopify carries Tobi's vision of what commerce should be, and merchants who adopt it are subtly shaped by that vision. This makes the product designer's worldview one of the highest-leverage things in business — it propagates to every user.
