# The Beautiful Problem

The best career bet is finding a problem so deep you can never fully solve it — and even if you accidentally solve it, it has "plenty of enlightened problem children" (Karl Popper's phrase). The sub-problems keep multiplying, so going deeper is always more rewarding than going sideways.

This is Tobi Lütke's framing of why he's spent 20+ years on internet commerce and isn't bored. Commerce is a beautiful problem: every solution (online stores, payments, logistics, marketing, AI agents) opens new sub-problems that are equally fascinating. Jensen Huang's relationship with GPUs follows the same pattern — a single problem domain explored so deeply it became the foundation for an entirely new computing paradigm.

The opposite of a beautiful problem is a boring problem — one you can fully solve and must then pivot away from. Business books that tell you to go horizontal assume your core competency was having a stopwatch. If your competency was loving the problem, depth wins.
