# The Multiverse Is Real

The many-worlds interpretation of quantum mechanics is not a metaphysical speculation -- it is the straightforward, literal reading of the equations. When a photon passes through a double slit and produces an interference pattern, something must be interfering with it. That something is the photon's counterparts in other universes. Denying those universes while accepting the interference pattern is like accepting fossils while denying dinosaurs.

Deutsch's criterion for reality is explanatory: an entity is "real" if it features in our best explanation of observable phenomena. We cannot see other universes directly, but we cannot see dinosaurs either. Both are inferred from physical evidence via the best available explanation. The Copenhagen interpretation -- which says quantum mechanics predicts outcomes but you must not ask what is actually happening between measurements -- is not a rival explanation. It is a refusal to explain.

The multiverse is not a pile of disconnected worlds. It is a vast, mostly autonomous structure where nearby universes resemble each other closely (the way nearby frames in a film resemble each other) and diverge at moments of quantum-mechanical differentiation. Time, causation, and even free will acquire physical meaning when understood as properties of this structure.

## Why it matters

Taking the multiverse seriously changes everything. It resolves the measurement problem, makes time travel paradoxes dissolve (you travel to a different universe's past), provides the physical substrate for quantum computation, and -- most surprisingly -- makes free will compatible with physics. In the multiverse, "I could have chosen otherwise" has a literal physical meaning: in nearby universes, my counterpart did choose otherwise.

The multiverse also provides the physical basis for understanding knowledge as complexity that extends across many universes. A photographic negative, which stores information, differs from a blank one precisely in that its state varies across universes in a structured way.
