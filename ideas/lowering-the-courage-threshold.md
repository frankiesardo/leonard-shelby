# Lowering the Courage Threshold

Instead of demanding more courage from people, design systems that require less of it. Trial runs, lower stakes, reversibility — these expand the pool of people who can create, start businesses, disagree with their boss, or post creative work.

Tobi Lütke cites Instagram's "trial run" feature (posting a reel to strangers before your followers see it) as one of the most profound pieces of software he's seen. It doesn't make people braver; it makes bravery less necessary. The same principle applies to Shopify itself: by handling the infrastructure, it lowers the courage threshold for starting a business from "quit your job and learn to code" to "spend a weekend setting up a store."

Every system that makes it easier to try and cheaper to fail is expanding human potential. The design implication: courage is the bottleneck for human output, so products that reduce the courage cost have outsized impact.
