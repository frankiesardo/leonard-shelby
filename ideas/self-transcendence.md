# Self-Transcendence

Being human always points, and is directed, to something or someone other than oneself -- a meaning to fulfill or another person to love. Frankl calls this "the self-transcendence of human existence" and considers it a constitutive characteristic of being human, not an optional virtue.

The counterintuitive consequence: self-actualization is not an attainable aim. The more you strive for it directly, the more you miss it. Self-actualization is only possible as a *side-effect* of self-transcendence. The more you forget yourself by giving yourself to a cause to serve or a person to love, the more human you become and the more you actualize yourself.

This is the same logic that governs happiness, success, and pleasure in Frankl's system. None can be pursued directly. All must ensue as the unintended by-product of dedication to something beyond the self. The person who chases happiness becomes less happy. The person who chases success becomes less successful. The person who chases self-actualization becomes less actual.

Frankl uses the analogy of the eye: the eye that sees itself is diseased (a cataract produces floaters). A healthy eye is transparent -- it functions precisely by *not* being aware of itself. The same is true of the self: the self that obsessively examines itself is stuck. The self that looks outward -- toward meaning, toward others -- functions properly.

## Why it matters

Self-transcendence is the mechanism that connects all the other Franklian ideas. The [will to meaning](will-to-meaning.md) is the drive; self-transcendence is how that drive is fulfilled. The [existential vacuum](the-existential-vacuum.md) is what happens when self-transcendence fails and people turn inward. [Paradoxical intention](paradoxical-intention.md) works through a specific form of self-transcendence -- dereflection redirects attention from the self to the proper object.

In clinical practice, "the cue to cure is self-transcendence." A neurotic's self-concern -- whether pity or contempt -- perpetuates the vicious circle. Only by looking beyond the self does the circle break.
