# Vertical Depth Over Horizontal Expansion

The Silicon Valley playbook overvalues "second acts" — launching unrelated business lines (the AWS model). The counterexample is NVIDIA: Jensen Huang spent an entire career going deeper on GPUs, not sideways into new TAMs, and it became the most valuable company in the world.

Depth compounds in ways that breadth doesn't. Understanding a domain deeply means the surface area of what's possible keeps expanding without you needing to change direction. VCs rejected Shopify in 2008 because "there are only 40,000 online stores in the world" — missing that Shopify itself was the solution to that small market. The problem was deep, not narrow.

Business books tell you to go horizontal because the core competency of most businesses was having a stopwatch — that's commoditized now. If your core competency is genuine domain understanding, going vertical is the better bet because you're building on compounding knowledge, not on a replicable process.
