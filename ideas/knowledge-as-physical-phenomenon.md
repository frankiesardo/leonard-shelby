# Knowledge as Physical Phenomenon

Hawking called the human race "chemical scum on a moderate-sized planet." Deutsch systematically demolishes this view. Knowledge -- in the physics sense of complexity that extends across many universes in a structured way -- is a fundamental physical quantity, not a parochial side-effect of biology.

Life is a form of virtual-reality generation: genes are programs that render organisms as environments for their own replication. But human knowledge goes qualitatively further. It creates explanations that can, in principle, reach to the entire fabric of reality. A single human mind can model the interior of a star or the structure of the multiverse -- physical regimes utterly unlike anything our ancestors encountered. No other known physical process has this property.

The laws of physics, by conforming to the Turing principle, mandate the physical existence of entities that understand them. Intelligence is not an accidental by-product; it is written into the structure of reality. In the long run, the behaviour of stars and galaxies will depend on the actions of intelligent beings who steer cosmic processes to sustain the growth of knowledge. The universe will, in the end, consist literally of intelligent thought-processes.

## Why it matters

This is the most optimistic thesis in the book. It inverts the standard scientific worldview that places humans on the periphery. We are not chemical scum. We are the means by which the universe understands itself. This is not mysticism -- it follows from taking the Turing principle and Popperian epistemology seriously as physics.

It also means that the growth of knowledge cannot be predicted. Since the future depends on what we will discover, and we cannot predict our own future discoveries (because then we would already possess that knowledge), the future is genuinely open. This is why Deutsch rejects prophecy: neither optimistic nor pessimistic predictions about the far future are justified, because they all assume that the growth of knowledge can be foreseen.
