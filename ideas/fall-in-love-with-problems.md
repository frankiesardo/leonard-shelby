# Fall in Love with Problems, Not Solutions

People who attach to the problem adapt when the landscape shifts; people who attach to their current solution become obstacles. The difference between problem-lovers and solution-lovers is invisible during stable times — they look exactly the same. It only surfaces when things change: platform shifts, reorgs, new technology.

Tobi Lütke uses this as a leadership litmus test. He deliberately induces change (reorgs, early adoption mandates) partly to identify which camp people are in. The problem-lover's response to disruption is curiosity; the solution-lover's response is defense. At Shopify's scale, the difference compounds enormously — a team of problem-lovers will pivot to AI-native commerce without resistance, while solution-lovers will spend months defending their existing architecture.
