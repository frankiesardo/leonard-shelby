# Freedom vs Equality

Freedom and equality are sworn and everlasting enemies. When one prevails, the other dies.

Leave men free, and their natural inequalities multiply almost geometrically — in talent, effort, luck, and compounding advantage. This is what happened in England and America in the nineteenth century under laissez-faire. To check inequality, liberty must be sacrificed — as in Russia after 1917. But even when repressed, inequality grows; only the man below average in economic ability desires equality; those conscious of superior ability desire freedom; and in the end superior ability has its way.

The Durants conclude that "utopias of equality are biologically doomed." The best an honest society can hope for is approximate equality of legal justice and educational opportunity — not equality of outcomes. A society that allows all potential abilities to develop will have a survival advantage in the competition of groups.

## Why it matters

This tension is not a bug in political philosophy — it is the fundamental tradeoff. Every policy choice, every institutional design, every revolution sits somewhere on the freedom-equality spectrum. The question is never "which one?" but "how much of each, given current conditions?" Understanding the tradeoff prevents the naive expectation that you can maximize both simultaneously.
