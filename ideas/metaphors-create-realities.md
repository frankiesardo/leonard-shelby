# Metaphors Create Realities

Metaphors do not merely describe pre-existing realities -- they bring new realities into being. When Carter declared the energy crisis "the moral equivalent of war," the WAR metaphor generated a network of entailments: an enemy, a threat to national security, targets, strategy, sacrifices, chains of command. These were not just words. They licensed policy changes, political actions, and economic decisions. The metaphor created a reality that people then lived in and acted upon.

This works because metaphors are self-reinforcing. When you start to comprehend your experience in terms of a metaphor, you act in accordance with it. Your actions then generate experiences that fit the metaphor, which strengthens it further. ARGUMENT IS WAR leads you to argue combatively, which produces combative responses, which confirms that arguments are war. LOVE IS A JOURNEY leads you to evaluate your relationship in terms of progress, direction, and destination -- and to feel stuck or lost when these terms don't apply.

The CHEMICAL metaphor that an Iranian student accidentally discovered in Berkeley is a vivid example. He heard "the solution of my problems" and imagined a chemical solution -- a liquid in which problems dissolve or precipitate out, never permanently disappearing, only changing form. This is a profoundly different reality from the PUZZLE metaphor most of us live by, where problems are solved once and for all. Living by the CHEMICAL metaphor would mean accepting that problems recur, that temporary dissolution is an accomplishment, and that the reappearance of an old problem is natural rather than a personal failure.

"Much of cultural change arises from the introduction of new metaphorical concepts and the loss of old ones." The Westernization of cultures worldwide is partly the spread of TIME IS MONEY into societies that previously had no such metaphor. When a culture adopts TIME IS MONEY, it begins to pay people by the hour, charge interest, and treat punctuality as a moral virtue. The metaphor creates the reality.

## Why it matters

If you want to change how people think, change the metaphors they use. This is the core mechanism of political rhetoric, marketing, therapy, and leadership. Framing a budget cut as "belt-tightening" versus "amputation" produces radically different emotional and practical responses, not because the facts differ but because the metaphors highlight different aspects and license different actions.

It also means that "people in power get to impose their metaphors" -- and the metaphors they impose become the reality that everyone else inhabits. This makes metaphor analysis a form of political literacy.
