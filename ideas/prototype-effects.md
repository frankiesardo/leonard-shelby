# Prototype Effects

Prototype effects are the empirical finding that people judge some members of a category as "better examples" than others — robins are better birds than penguins, desk chairs are better chairs than beanbags, 3 is a better odd number than 23. The finding is robust across cultures, tasks, and domains, and it directly challenges the classical theory that categories are defined by properties shared equally by all members.

The critical insight from Lakoff's *Women, Fire, and Dangerous Things*: **prototype effects are not category structure.** Goodness-of-example ratings reflect how well something fits an [idealized cognitive model](idealized-cognitive-models.md), not degrees of membership. A penguin is fully a bird — 100% a bird — but it's a poor *example* because it doesn't fly, sing, or perch, and our bird ICM is organized around those features.

## Why it matters

This distinction — effects vs. structure — resolves what looked like a fatal problem for prototype theory. Critics (Osherson & Smith, Armstrong, Gleitman, & Gleitman) showed that even classically defined categories like "odd number" produce prototype effects: people judge 3 as a "better" odd number than 447. If prototype effects reflected degrees of membership, this would be absurd — all odd numbers are equally odd. But under Lakoff's ICM interpretation, it makes perfect sense: we understand numbers through multiple cognitive models (base-10, multiples of 5, powers of 2), and single-digit numbers are privileged across all these models. The odd-even distinction is all-or-nothing, but our *understanding* of numbers produces graded judgments.

Prototype effects arise from several sources:
- **Metonymic models**: Stereotypes, ideals, paragons, and typical cases stand for the whole category
- **Scalar models**: Some ICMs have built-in gradations (e.g., tallness)
- **Imperfect ICM fit**: The ICM is idealized; reality matches it to varying degrees
- **Radial structure**: Central members fit the ICM directly; peripheral members are linked by extension
