# The Formula of Humanity

Kant's ethical principle: "Act that you use humanity, whether in your own person or in the person of any other, always at the same time as an end, never merely as a means."

Manson frames this as the single principle that pulls people out of adolescent bargaining and into adult virtue. The core insight is that hope is fundamentally *transactional* -- a bargain between present suffering and imagined future gain. To transcend hope, you must act *unconditionally*: love without expecting return, be honest without expecting praise, show courage without calculating reward.

The Formula of Humanity works because consciousness is the only truly scarce and unique thing in the known universe. It is the only force that can conceive of value, create meaning, and direct its own fate. Therefore, consciousness itself is the supreme value -- the thing from which all other values derive. To treat any conscious being (including yourself) merely as a means to some other end is to violate the only thing that gives the universe significance.

This explains our deepest moral intuitions:
- **Lying** is wrong because you manipulate another's consciousness to serve your ends.
- **Violence** is wrong because you treat another person as a means to political or personal gain.
- **Honesty** is good in itself because it is the only communication that respects the other's autonomous reasoning.
- **Self-harm** is wrong because you treat your own consciousness as a means, not an end.

The Formula also provides a framework for psychological maturity:
- **Childhood**: values based on pleasure and pain (the child as means to its own gratification).
- **Adolescence**: values based on rules and transactions (everything is a bargain, everyone is a means to some end).
- **Adulthood**: values based on unconditional principles (people are ends in themselves; the virtues of honesty, courage, and humility are good for their own sake).

The beauty of this framework is that it requires no hope. There is no utopia to achieve, no ideology to enforce. There is only the choice, in each moment, to treat yourself and others as ends or as means.
