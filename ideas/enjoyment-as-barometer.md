# Enjoyment as Barometer

If a naturally evolved human activity — speaking, moving, creating — stops feeling enjoyable, that's a diagnostic signal you're doing it wrong, not proof that the activity is inherently unpleasant. Humans are built for communication; it's naturally rewarding when we feel like ourselves. When speaking stops being fun, you're overthinking.

This applies equally to practice: if your practice isn't enjoyable, you'll quit in two weeks, just like a bad fitness program. The best practice is designed to be engaging *in itself*, not just useful for some future payoff.
