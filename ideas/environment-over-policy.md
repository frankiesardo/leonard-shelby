# Environment Over Policy

The standard levers for running a company — incentives and policies — are weaker than the one people overlook: the tools and physical/digital environment people use every day. Changes to internal software deploy instantly and reshape behavior faster than any town hall rollout could. Marshall McLuhan's "first we make the tools, then they shape us" applied literally.

At Shopify, Tobi Lütke built an internal system called GSD ("Getting Shit Done") that acts as the company's operating environment. When it changes, behavior changes — not because anyone sent a memo, but because the paths of least resistance shifted. The meeting rooms are placed in dark interior spaces so people don't spend all day in meetings. Pods that seat five uncomfortably fit seven — no policy needed for team size. Instead of corporate babyproofing (policies that tell people to override their intuition), design the environment so the right thing is intuitive.

This is distinct from personal environment design (putting your guitar in the living room). This is organizational: the software you choose, the office you design, the tools you build internally — these shape behavior more powerfully and more quickly than incentives, policies, or cultural exhortation.
