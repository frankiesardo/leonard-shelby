# Exothermic Leadership

Companies at room temperature don't outperform. Founders and the best leaders are "exothermic" — they inject heat into the system. When Tobi Lütke asks "what is your energy source?", he's asking what fuels that heat. His own: dissatisfaction with the status quo, the conviction that today is the dystopia of the future. Michael Jordan's: rivalry and manufactured insult.

The critical insight for founder mode: the organizational immune system fights the heat-injectors. People try to take them out, cool them down, normalize them. Founder-led companies stay innovative longer because the founder can protect the other exothermic people from the immune system. This is the emotional substrate underneath all the systems thinking — you can model the org computationally, but someone has to care enough to keep injecting heat.
