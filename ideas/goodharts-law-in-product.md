# Goodhart's Law in Product

Any metric that becomes a goal ceases to be a useful metric. Shopify has no OKRs and no KPIs in the traditional sense — not because they don't measure things, but because they treat dashboards as cockpit instruments, not autopilot. The pilot makes the decisions; the instruments inform them.

The overlap between the most valuable things you can do with a product and the things that happen to be fully quantifiable is maybe 20%. Schools overfit for marks, ML models overfit for loss functions, and businesses overfit for metrics. Same phenomenon, different terminology. The things that matter most — fun, delight, taste, craft — are upstream of every metric that business culture worships, but they resist quantification. Optimizing the metric directly is the product-level version of the overfitting problem.

Shopify invests heavily in data infrastructure — dashboards that let you decompose any cohort in minutes — but the data serves taste, not the other way around.
