# P/PC Balance

Effectiveness is a function of two things: **Production** (P) — the golden eggs — and **Production Capability** (PC) — the goose that lays them. Neglect the goose and the eggs stop. Kill the goose for all the eggs at once and you get nothing. This is Aesop's fable turned into an operating principle.

The balance applies to three kinds of assets:
- **Physical**: Run a machine without maintenance and it breaks. Run your body without rest and it breaks.
- **Financial**: Invade principal to boost lifestyle and the principal shrinks. Neglect your ability to earn and you become economically dependent.
- **Human**: Neglect a relationship to maximize short-term output and the relationship withers. Then the output stops too.

Covey's Habit 7 — "Sharpen the Saw" — is the PC habit. Four dimensions of renewal: physical (exercise, nutrition, rest), spiritual (values, meditation, nature), mental (reading, writing, planning), and social/emotional (service, empathy, deep relationships). The **Daily Private Victory** — one hour a day invested in the first three — is the single most powerful application.

## Why it matters

Most failures of effectiveness are P/PC imbalances. The manager who squeezes the team for quarterly results while turnover eats the foundation. The entrepreneur who works 80-hour weeks but never exercises, reads, or maintains friendships. The parent who demands obedience but never invests in understanding.

The imbalance always looks rational in the short term. There's always a reason to skip the run, cancel the one-on-one, postpone the reading. But the compounding cost is invisible until the goose is dead.

The inverse is also true: pure PC without P is self-indulgent. You can sharpen the saw forever and never cut anything. The balance is the point.
