# The Happiness Formula

H = S + C + V. The level of happiness you actually experience (H) is determined by your biological setpoint (S) plus the conditions of your life (C) plus the voluntary activities (V) you do. This formula, developed by Lyubomirsky, Sheldon, Schkade, and Seligman, is the central framework of positive psychology's approach to well-being.

The biological setpoint (S) is largely genetic. Twin studies show that roughly 50% of the variation in happiness is heritable. Each person has a characteristic range, and genes influence whether you operate at the top or bottom of that range. You cannot change your setpoint, but you are not imprisoned by it either -- it is a range, not a fixed number.

The conditions of your life (C) are the external circumstances: wealth, marital status, age, health, climate, noise, commute. The surprising finding is that most conditions matter very little because of the [hedonic treadmill](the-hedonic-treadmill.md). But some conditions resist adaptation: strong relationships, control over stressors, freedom from chronic noise and long commutes, and freedom from shame about one's appearance. These are worth striving for.

Voluntary activities (V) are the things you choose to do: meditation, exercise, gratitude practices, acts of kindness, pursuing flow experiences, using your character strengths. These resist adaptation because they require ongoing effort and attention -- they cannot fade into the background the way conditions do. The Buddha and the Stoics focused almost entirely on V (cultivate detachment). Haidt argues they were right to emphasize V but wrong to dismiss C entirely.

## Why it matters

The formula resolves a debate that has lasted millennia. The Eastern sages said happiness is purely internal (H = S + V). Western strivers act as though it is purely external (H = C). The truth is that both matter, but V matters most because voluntary activities are the most resistant to adaptation and the most within your control. The formula also gives you a practical diagnostic: if you are unhappy, check all three factors. Is your setpoint being dragged down by untreated depression or anxiety (fix S with therapy or medication)? Are your life conditions genuinely bad in ways that resist adaptation (fix C by changing jobs, relationships, or living situation)? Or are you simply not doing enough of the activities that produce lasting well-being (fix V by adding gratitude, kindness, flow, and strength-based activities to your day)?
