# The Braintrust

The Braintrust is Pixar's mechanism for giving honest, expert feedback on films in progress. A group of experienced storytellers -- directors, writers, heads of story -- watches a rough cut and tells the director what isn't working. The critical design constraint: the Braintrust has no authority. It makes no prescriptions. The director is free to ignore every note.

This is what makes it different from executive notes, which are feedback backed by power. When your boss gives you a note, it's an instruction disguised as a suggestion. When a peer with no authority gives you a note, it's just information. You can weigh it on its merits.

The Braintrust operates on a few principles: you are not your idea; a frank assessment of a work in progress is a kindness, not an attack; and the goal is always to make the movie better, never to prove you're smarter than the director. People learn to give notes on the problem, not to prescribe solutions. "Here's where I got confused" is a Braintrust note. "You should change the second act to X" is not.

## Why it matters

Most organizations have feedback mechanisms that are either toothless (everyone is polite, nothing changes) or authoritarian (the boss decides, everyone complies). The Braintrust threads the needle: it generates genuinely useful feedback by removing the two things that usually corrupt feedback -- deference and ego.

The removal of authority is not incidental. It's the whole design. If you give the feedback group power over the project, two things happen: the director stops listening honestly (they're now managing their boss, not processing information), and the feedback group starts hedging (they'll be responsible if their notes don't work). Remove the authority and both sides can be honest.

Catmull notes that the Braintrust only works when trust is high. When Braintrust sessions deteriorate, it's usually because someone is playing politics, protecting themselves, or trying to seem smart rather than trying to help. The mechanism requires constant maintenance.
