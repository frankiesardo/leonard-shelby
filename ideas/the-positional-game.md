# The Positional Game

The business world only talks about tactics: A/B tests, conversion rate changes, growth hacks. But the real value accrues from the positional game — territory, trust, role, product surface area, how deeply customers rely on you.

The chess analogy from Tobi Lütke: tactics extract value from your position. If you extract all of it, you have nothing left. The companies that "got to a point and then faded" were tacticked out of their position. They optimized so aggressively for short-term gains that they depleted the foundation those gains were built on.

The founder's job is to play the positional game and create finite, winnable games for the team that serve the infinite game of the mission. Shopify's partnership with Stripe is an example: both companies play the positional game (expanding the total market) rather than the tactical game (fighting over share).
