# Watering Seeds

Thich Nhat Hanh's teaching, relayed by Kornfield: in the heart and mind there are all kinds of seeds. Seeds of joy, love, connection, creativity, peace. And seeds of fear, anxiety, anger, grasping. How you live depends on which seeds you water.

This is not passive positive thinking. It's active cultivation. You water a seed by recognizing it's there and then heading your mind in its direction — through practice, through repetition, through the deliberate choice to spend time with certain thoughts and let others pass. The unwholesome seeds don't need to be attacked or uprooted. They just need to stop being fed.

Kornfield teaches a practical technique: number your Top 10 Tunes — the recurring thought patterns that dominate your meditation (and your life). "Number one: what's happening with my daughter. Number two: the stock portfolio. Number three: the creative project I can't let go of." When one comes up, you say "oh, number three, thank you" and let it pass. The numbering creates enough distance to recognize the pattern without being swallowed by it.

## Why it matters

Most people water their fear seeds unconsciously — through habitual worry, rumination, doom-scrolling, rehashing arguments. The mechanism is invisible because 90% of thoughts are repeats. You're running the same playlist day after day without realizing it. Making the playlist conscious is the first step. Choosing which tracks to play is the second.

This framework bridges the gap between the Buddhist insight (attachment causes suffering) and the practical question (okay, but what do I *do*?). You do this: notice which seeds are growing, stop feeding the ones that don't serve you, deliberately tend the ones that do.

## Connections

- Rohn's [Guard the Door of Your Mind](guard-the-door-of-your-mind.md) is the same principle from the self-development tradition — curate your inputs and the outputs change. Rohn focuses on external inputs (what you read, who you spend time with); Thich Nhat Hanh focuses on internal cultivation (which mental patterns you reinforce)
- [Identity-Based Habits](identity-based-habits.md) — Clear's insight that every action is a vote for the identity you're becoming. Watering seeds is the contemplative version: every thought you follow reinforces the person you're becoming
- [The Backwards Law](the-backwards-law.md) — you can't water seeds of peace by aggressively attacking seeds of anxiety. The attacking *is* more anxiety
- [Nonjudgmental Awareness](nonjudgmental-awareness.md) is the prerequisite — you have to see the seeds clearly before you can choose which to water
- [Happiness as Skill](happiness-as-skill.md) — watering seeds is one of the core training methods for the skill
