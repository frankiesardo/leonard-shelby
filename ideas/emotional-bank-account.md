# Emotional Bank Account

Every relationship has a metaphorical bank account that tracks trust. Positive interactions — keeping commitments, showing understanding, being honest — are deposits. Negative ones — breaking promises, disrespect, duplicity — are withdrawals. The balance determines the quality of everything that flows through the relationship.

With a high balance, communication is easy. You can say something clumsy and people give you the benefit of the doubt. You can make a mistake and it gets absorbed. With an overdrawn account, every word is suspect. Even well-intended gestures are interpreted through a lens of distrust.

Covey identifies six major deposits:
1. **Understanding the individual** — what matters to them might not be what matters to you
2. **Attending to little things** — small courtesies and kindnesses compound
3. **Keeping commitments** — a broken promise is a massive withdrawal
4. **Clarifying expectations** — ambiguity breeds disappointment
5. **Showing personal integrity** — be loyal to the absent; treat everyone by the same principles
6. **Apologizing sincerely when you make a withdrawal** — a genuine apology is itself a deposit

## Why it matters

The metaphor is powerful because it explains asymmetries that feel irrational on the surface. Why does your partner erupt over a small thing? Because the account was already overdrawn — the small thing was the last withdrawal, not the cause. Why can one team lead get away with direct feedback while another can't? Because one has a surplus of trust and the other doesn't.

The deposit that matters most is #1 — understanding. What counts as a deposit to one person might be a withdrawal to another. Taking your teenager fishing is only a deposit if fishing matters to *them*. Covey's daughter didn't want a generic "date night" — she wanted to show him Star Wars. His willingness to enter her world, on her terms, was the real deposit.

Integrity (#5) is the hardest and most consequential. Being loyal to the absent — not talking about someone behind their back — is a deposit with *everyone* in the room, because they now know you won't do it to them either. Duplicity — being kind to someone's face and critical behind their back — is a withdrawal from every relationship simultaneously.
