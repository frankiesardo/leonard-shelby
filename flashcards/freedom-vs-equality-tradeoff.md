<summary>The Durants say "freedom and equality are sworn and everlasting enemies." What do they mean, and what is the best achievable outcome?</summary>

Leave men free, and their natural inequalities multiply geometrically — in talent, effort, luck, and compounding advantage. To check inequality, liberty must be sacrificed. You cannot maximize both simultaneously.

The best achievable outcome is approximate equality of **legal justice** and **educational opportunity** — not equality of result. A society that allows all potential abilities to develop will have a survival advantage in the competition of groups.

"Utopias of equality are biologically doomed."

See: [Freedom vs Equality](../ideas/freedom-vs-equality.md)
