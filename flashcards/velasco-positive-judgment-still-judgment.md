<summary>Velasco's hitters learned not to criticize the set. Instead they say "perfect!" on good sets. Why does Velasco still object?</summary>

Because positive judgment is still judgment. Saying "perfect!" on good sets implicitly communicates that other sets were not perfect. The hitter is still focused on evaluating the setter's work instead of solving their own task. Any evaluation — positive or negative — keeps Self 1 in the judging role. Gallwey discovered the same thing: complimenting a player's footwork immediately made it worse, because the compliment set a standard that activated Self 1's monitoring.

See: [Nonjudgmental Awareness](../ideas/nonjudgmental-awareness.md), [Separation of Tasks](../ideas/separation-of-tasks.md)
