<summary>In Haidt's rider-and-elephant metaphor, who evolved to serve whom -- and what evidence from brain-damaged patients proves the point?</summary>

The rider (conscious reasoning) evolved to serve the elephant (emotions, automatic processes), not the other way around. Language and controlled processing are evolutionary newcomers -- like buggy software running on ancient, refined hardware.

The proof comes from Antonio Damasio's patients who lost emotional capacity through damage to the orbitofrontal cortex. Their reasoning and logic remained intact -- they performed normally on intelligence tests. But without the elephant's emotional guidance, they could not make even simple decisions. They would deliberate endlessly over trivial choices because nothing *felt* better or worse than anything else. Without emotion, reason is paralyzed.

This means willpower alone cannot change behavior. The rider tires like a muscle; the elephant runs on automatic, effortlessly and endlessly. To change yourself, you must train the elephant through habit, repetition, and environmental design -- not lecture it.
