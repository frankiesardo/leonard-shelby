<summary>Why should you hire the "4-and-2" candidate over the "3-out-of-4" candidate?</summary>

The candidate who scores four 4s and four 2s has clear strengths that can create differentiation, while the uniformly-good 3-out-of-4 candidate produces adequate but undifferentiated work. Weaknesses are manageable; mediocrity is not. The same principle applies to team composition — the spikiest teams (Grateful Dead, 2004 Red Sox) create new categories by combining radically different strengths.
