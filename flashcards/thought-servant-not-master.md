<summary>Kornfield says "thought is a great servant -- it's just not a good master." What does he mean, and how does this connect to the Self 1 / Self 2 framework?</summary>

Ninety percent of your thoughts are repeats. Sitting in meditation reveals a mind that's like "being stuck in Motel 6 at night with the shopping channel on." These thoughts aren't new insights -- they're the brain reciting certain patterns on autopilot.

The move is not to stop thinking (that's temporary and not the goal), but to stop letting thought run your life. You want presence and compassion to be the master, with thought as the servant. This means being able to notice a thought, say "thank you, I appreciate it, that's what minds do," and then tune into a deeper quality of being present.

This maps directly onto the Self 1 / Self 2 framework: Self 1 (the verbal, judging mind) is a great servant when it feeds observations to Self 2 (the body, the unconscious intelligence). It's a terrible master when it runs the show -- micromanaging, judging, replaying the same worries. Kornfield's meditation training is, in Gallwey's terms, teaching Self 1 to serve rather than command.
