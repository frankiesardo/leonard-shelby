<summary>What are Manson's three criteria that distinguish good values from bad values? Give an example of each.</summary>

**Good values** are:
1. **Reality-based / evidence-based** (not superstitious)
2. **Socially constructive** (not destructive)
3. **Immediate and controllable** (not dependent on external outcomes)

Example: *honesty* — it's based in reality, it builds trust, and you can practice it right now regardless of what anyone else does.

**Bad values** are superstitious, socially destructive, and not controllable.

Example: *being liked by everybody* — it's based on others' subjective reactions, it incentivizes people-pleasing and dishonesty, and it depends entirely on external validation you can't control.

The key difference: good values are process-oriented (never "complete"), while bad values are outcome-oriented (fragile once achieved or impossible to achieve).

See: [Good and Bad Values](../ideas/good-and-bad-values.md)
