<summary>In what sense does Deutsch claim that all experience is "virtual reality," and what does this imply about the relationship between science and imagination?</summary>

We never experience reality directly. The brain is locked in a dark, silent skull and generates a virtual-reality rendering from sensory data plus inborn and acquired theories about interpretation. Mirages, optical illusions, the apparent stillness of the Earth, and our experience of a single universe are all artifacts of this rendering. Science, mathematics, imagination, and fiction are therefore all forms of virtual-reality generation -- they all involve the physical embodying of a theory about an environment. The difference is that science demands its renderings be testable against reality, while art and fiction do not. The ecological niche of the human species *is* virtual reality, as fundamentally as eucalyptus leaves define the koala's niche.
