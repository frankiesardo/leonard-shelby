<summary>What are idealized cognitive models (ICMs), and what are the four types?</summary>

ICMs are structured, idealized representations of domains of experience that we use to organize knowledge. Categories are defined relative to ICMs, and prototype effects are by-products of how well things fit the relevant models. The four types are:

1. **Propositional** — frame-like structures with roles and relations (e.g., the "restaurant" frame)
2. **Image-schematic** — spatial patterns from bodily experience (CONTAINER, PATH, LINK, BALANCE)
3. **Metaphoric** — systematic mappings from concrete to abstract domains (TIME IS MONEY)
4. **Metonymic** — part-for-whole or subcategory-for-category substitutions (stereotypes, paragons)
