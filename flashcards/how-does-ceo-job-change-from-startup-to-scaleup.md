<summary>How does the CEO's job change from startup to scale-up, and what's the primary skill gap?</summary>

Startup is 90% perspiration, 10% inspiration. Scale-up inverts to 90% inspiration, 10% perspiration. The CEO shifts from doing every job to hiring, inspiring, and making one-way door decisions quickly. The primary skill gap: giving hard feedback. Most young founders have never had to tell a VP they're underperforming, layer someone, or manage a co-founder transition. This unnatural skill is the number one thing CEOs must learn.

See: [Constructive Dissatisfaction](../ideas/constructive-dissatisfaction.md)
