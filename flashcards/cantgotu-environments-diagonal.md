**Q:** What are "Cantgotu environments" and what does their existence prove about the limits of computation?

**A:** Cantgotu environments (named as a portmanteau of Cantor, Godel, and Turing) are logically possible environments that no physically possible virtual-reality generator can render. The proof uses the diagonal argument: list every possible program; each generates an environment. Define a new environment that differs from Environment N during minute N. This environment cannot be on the list, so no program can generate it. For every renderable environment, there are infinitely many Cantgotu environments. This establishes an absolute, permanent boundary: the overwhelming majority of logically possible truths are non-computable. But within that boundary, the Turing principle guarantees that everything physically possible *is* renderable. The boundary is a deep feature of the fabric of reality, not a limitation of current technology.
