<summary>Name three desirable difficulties and explain why they improve learning despite impairing short-term performance.</summary>

1. **Generation effect:** Struggling to produce an answer before seeing it -- even getting it wrong -- enhances later retention. The more confident you are in a wrong answer, the better you learn the right one (hypercorrection effect).

2. **Spacing:** Leaving time between practice sessions. Spanish vocabulary learners tested a month after learning retained 250% more eight years later than those tested the same day.

3. **Interleaving:** Mixing problem types during practice. Students perform worse during interleaved practice but better on tests, because mixed practice forces them to identify the problem type first -- the most important real-world step.

All three impair short-term performance, which is why they feel wrong. But for durable, transferable knowledge, "fast and easy is precisely the problem."
