<summary>What goes wrong when two people share responsibility for an initiative? What's the fix?</summary>

The initiative either gets overdone (both people act independently) or underdone (both people defer, each assuming the other is handling it). There is no middle outcome. The fix is single-threaded ownership: one DRI (Directly Responsible Individual) with enough authority to direct people across functions. As Halligan puts it: "If you want to kill a plant, have two people water it."
