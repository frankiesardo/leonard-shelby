<summary>What is MAYA (Maximally Advanced Yet Acceptable) and what failure mode does it prevent?</summary>

Raymond Loewy's principle: there's a pace of change consumers will absorb. Push past it and they reject the product — not because it's bad, but because it's too unfamiliar. Loewy made a fan so quiet people didn't believe it worked. The iPhone succeeded partly because the iPod preceded it — consumers migrated rather than leapt. MAYA prevents the painful failure mode where you build something genuinely better and the market rejects it anyway.
