**Q:** What is a character's "sacred flaw" in Storr's framework, and why does it generate drama?

**A:** The sacred flaw is a broken belief about the world that the character has made central to their identity. It simultaneously gives them their power (it is their superpower, their strategy for controlling the world) and does them damage (it warps their perception and harms their relationships). It generates drama because: (1) the character cannot see it, since it is built into their model of reality, (2) the plot is designed to test and break it, and (3) fixing it means breaking down the very structure of their perception and rebuilding it -- which is why we call those who manage it heroes.
