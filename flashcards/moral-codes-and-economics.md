<summary>The Durants argue moral codes follow economic systems. What are the three eras they trace, and what are we living through now?</summary>

Three moral eras corresponding to three economic systems:

1. **Hunting stage** — Pugnacity, greed, and sexual readiness were survival advantages. "Probably every vice was once a virtue."
2. **Agricultural stage** — Industriousness over bravery, thrift over violence, chastity and monogamy as economic necessities. This code lasted 1,500 years in Christian Europe.
3. **Industrial stage** — The Industrial Revolution undermined the agricultural code. Economic maturity comes later, marriage is delayed, cities provide every stimulus for sex and every discouragement for family, the authority of parents loses its economic base.

We are in the **interregnum** — the relaxing interval between one moral code and the next. The agricultural code has lost its economic basis, but we haven't yet forged the industrial replacement. The Durants take comfort in noting that civilizations decay leisurely: Greece produced masterpieces for 250 years after moral weakening began.
