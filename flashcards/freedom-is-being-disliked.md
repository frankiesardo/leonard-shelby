<summary>Adler says "freedom is being disliked by other people." This sounds counterintuitive. What does he actually mean, and how does it connect to separation of tasks?</summary>

The desire to be liked is a natural inclination -- but following it without resistance is slavery, not freedom. A stone rolling downhill is obeying gravity; a person who shapes every action around others' approval is obeying the same kind of force.

**Real freedom** is the willingness to be disliked -- not wanting to be disliked, but not fearing it. Whether someone likes you is *their task*, not yours. Trying to control their opinion is a task-boundary violation that costs you your autonomy.

"The courage to be happy also includes the courage to be disliked." The person who can pay the cost of disapproval can live according to their own principles. The person who cannot pays a higher cost: a life of performance and suppression.
