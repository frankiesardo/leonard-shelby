<summary>Why does Tobi Lütke prefer designing environments over posting policies?</summary>

Policies tell people to override their intuition. But there's a higher-order option: design the environment so the right thing *is* intuitive. Pods that comfortably seat five but uncomfortably fit seven -- no policy needed for team size. Meeting rooms in dark interior spaces -- people naturally don't spend all day in meetings. Norman doors (handles that mislead you about push vs. pull) are a design crime because they create affordances that make people predictably do the wrong thing. "Think about how much policy you don't have to post if the environment just makes you do the obvious thing."
