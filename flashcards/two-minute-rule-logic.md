<summary>Why should a new habit take less than two minutes to do, even if the real goal requires much more?</summary>

Because a habit must be established before it can be improved. You have to standardize before you can optimize. The Two-Minute Rule ("read one page," "tie my running shoes") masters the art of showing up -- the decisive moment that sets the trajectory for everything that follows. Once the habit of showing up is automatic, scaling up happens naturally. The man who only went to the gym for five minutes a day ended up losing over a hundred pounds. Showing up consistently for two minutes beats sporadic heroic efforts every time.
