<summary>What is a radial category, and why can't classical theory account for it?</summary>

A radial category has a central case and conventionalized extensions that are **motivated but not predictable**. The extensions make sense given the center, but you can't generate them by rule — each must be learned individually.

Example: In Dyirbal, the classifier *balan* groups women, fire, and dangerous things. The chain: women → sun (a female deity) → fire (associated with sun) → dangerous things (associated with fire). Each link is motivated, but the endpoints share no common properties.

Classical theory requires that all members share defining properties. Radial categories have no such properties — members are linked by chains of association, metaphor, and convention. This is the strongest evidence against the classical view and the strongest evidence for cognitive models.
