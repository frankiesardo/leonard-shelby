**Q:** What is the Conduit metaphor, and what does it hide about communication?

**A:** The Conduit metaphor structures how we think about language through three mappings: IDEAS ARE OBJECTS, LINGUISTIC EXPRESSIONS ARE CONTAINERS, and COMMUNICATION IS SENDING. The speaker puts ideas into words and sends them to a hearer who extracts the ideas. Reddy found this accounts for ~70% of English expressions about language ("pack more thought into fewer words," "the meaning is right there in the words"). It hides three critical things: (1) meaning depends on context -- "Please sit in the apple-juice seat" has no meaning in isolation but makes perfect sense at a breakfast table, (2) the same sentence means different things to different people -- "We need new alternative sources of energy" means opposite things to the CEO of Mobil Oil and the president of Friends of the Earth, and (3) understanding is active construction, not passive extraction.
