**Q:** What is the Blue Dot Effect, and why does it explain the paradox of progress?

**A:** When researchers reduced the number of blue dots shown to subjects, subjects began misidentifying purple dots as blue -- their minds maintained a preset expectation regardless of reality. The same held for threatening faces and unethical proposals. This means our perception adjusts to maintain a constant level of dissatisfaction: the better things objectively get, the more we redefine previously acceptable things as problems. This is why the safest, most prosperous era in history feels so broken.
