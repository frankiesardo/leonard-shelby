<summary>Why did the word processor make bad writers worse?</summary>

Because nobody told computer-age writers that the essence of writing is rewriting. The word processor made verbose sentences look beautiful on screen — clean fonts, perfect margins — creating the illusion of polished prose. Good writers got better (endless fussing became easy). Bad writers got worse (they mistook fluent production for quality). Writing well still requires the discipline of cutting and reshaping.
