<summary>Sutherland argues the Eurostar spent billions shaving 40 minutes off the London-Paris journey. What would a "psychological moonshot" have looked like instead?</summary>

Hire supermodels to serve free champagne, and passengers would have asked the train to go slower. The engineering solution was expensive and invisible to the passenger experience; the psychological solution would have been cheap and transformative. The principle: always ask both "What's the technological fix?" and "What's the psychological fix?" — the latter is usually cheaper, faster, and works with human nature instead of against it.
