<summary>Catmull proposes a simple diagnostic for organizational candor. What is it, and what does it reveal about the structure (not the people)?</summary>

"If there is more truth in the hallways than in meetings, you have a problem." The hallway conversation is candid because it's low-stakes and informal. The meeting is guarded because it's high-stakes and hierarchical. The gap between hallway truth and meeting truth is a structural indicator, not a character deficiency. It reveals that the organization hasn't built mechanisms that make candor safe in formal settings. The fix is structural (forums like the Braintrust, postmortems, Notes Day) rather than motivational ("just be more honest").
