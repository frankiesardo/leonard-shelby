<summary>Why is money more effective at enabling cooperation than religion, nationalism, or any other imagined order?</summary>

Because money requires the least buy-in. Christianity requires belief in Christ; nationalism requires loyalty to a specific nation; Marxism requires accepting a theory of history. Money asks only that you trust that *others* trust it. A Christian and a Muslim who share no beliefs, a Spanish conquistador and an Aztec merchant who share no language -- they can still cooperate through money. This minimal requirement of mutual trust (not shared values) makes money the most universal system of cooperation ever devised and the supreme unifier of humankind.
