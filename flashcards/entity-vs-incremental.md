<summary>What's the difference between entity and incremental theories of intelligence, and why does it matter for handling failure?</summary>

Entity theorists believe ability is fixed ("I'm smart"). Incremental theorists believe ability is built through effort ("I worked hard"). When both groups encounter failure, entity theorists collapse — the failure threatens their identity. Incremental theorists recover — the failure is just information about what to work on next. The framing determines whether setbacks become data points or identity crises.
