<summary>What is "resulting" and why does it prevent learning?</summary>

Resulting is the tendency to judge the quality of a decision by the quality of its outcome. A good decision can produce a bad result (bad luck) and a bad decision can produce a good result (good luck). When you conflate the two, you learn the wrong lessons — you abandon good strategies that happened to fail and reinforce bad strategies that happened to succeed.
