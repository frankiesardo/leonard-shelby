<summary>What is an "area under the curve" company and why does venture capital systematically undervalue them?</summary>

An area-under-the-curve company grows slowly but accumulates deep defensibility — network effects, workflow integration, technical depth — that hypercurve companies can't match. Figma is the example: years of apparent stagnation, then an N=1 network effect product that emerged fully defensible.

VC systematically undervalues these because portfolio reviews reward slope (growth rate now) over area (total value over time). A flat early phase looks like failure. Founders who understand the shape of their own curve — and don't panic-optimize for slope — can hold long enough to see the full value express.
