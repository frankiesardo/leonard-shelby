<summary>Why is "willingness to be embarrassed" a genuine competitive advantage for consumer founders?</summary>

Big corporations have committees designed to prevent their products from touching uncomfortable human territory — persuasion, sexuality, disagreement, intimacy. This creates a market gap for startups willing to build there.

Young founders with low status-anxiety can occupy that space freely. Post-exit founders often lose this: once you've sold a company, you care too much about sounding credible to bet on things that could be dismissed as silly. The flinch before committing to something embarrassing is exactly what kills the best consumer ideas before they start.
