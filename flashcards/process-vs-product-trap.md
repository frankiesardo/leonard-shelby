<summary>You're fixated on shipping the final article/feature/presentation. What's the risk, and what's the alternative?</summary>

Fixating on the product short-circuits the messy, uncertain middle where the real thinking happens. You skip exploration, avoid failure, and produce something that looks finished but wasn't thought through. The alternative: focus on process — explore, iterate, give yourself the right to fail. The product that emerges from a sound process is almost always better than one aimed at from the start.

See: [Process over Product](../ideas/process-over-product.md)

</details>
