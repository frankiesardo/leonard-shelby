<summary>What's the difference between rivalry and competition for companies, and why does it matter?</summary>

Competition leads to mimicry: watching what others build and copying it ("Xerox strategy"). Your most active Slack channel becomes competitive analysis. The result is convergence toward shared mediocrity. Rivalry inspires your best: you use the rival as a mirror to measure your own progress, not a template to copy. Agassi needed Sampras -- not to copy his game, but to be pushed beyond his own limits. Companies without rivals stagnate; companies that copy competitors converge.
