<summary>Why is "founder-capital fit" a critical failure mode, and what's the hidden third option when facing two bad choices?</summary>

Most founders given infinite capital make worse decisions — not from waste, but because unlimited runway removes the forcing function. When option A is bad and option B is bad, the hidden third option is making no choice at all. With enough money, you can afford to not decide: do 10 things instead of 2, none work, culture degrades. Necessity is the mother of invention; remove necessity, remove invention. The rare exception: founders with the Count of Monte Cristo motivation who make sharp decisions regardless of bank balance.
