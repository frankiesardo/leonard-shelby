<summary>In Peterson's reading, what does the Cain and Abel story reveal about the difference between meaningful sacrifice and resentful sacrifice?</summary>

Abel sacrifices the best of what he has willingly, and his offering is accepted -- things go well. Cain sacrifices reluctantly and poorly; his offering is rejected. Instead of examining his own sacrifice and improving it, Cain turns bitter and murders Abel. The lesson: meaningful sacrifice requires giving up something genuinely valued, voluntarily, for something higher. Resentful or half-hearted sacrifice -- doing the right thing while begrudging it -- is worse than useless. It breeds the kind of bitterness that turns destructive.
