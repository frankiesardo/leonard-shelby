<summary>What are the three biological lessons of history, according to the Durants?</summary>

1. **Life is competition.** Peaceful when food abounds, violent when mouths outrun food. Cooperation is real but is mostly a tool and form of competition — we cooperate within our group to compete against other groups.

2. **Life is selection.** Nature has not read the Declaration of Independence. We are all born unfree and unequal. Inequality is natural and grows with the complexity of civilization.

3. **Life must breed.** Nature has a passion for quantity as prerequisite to the selection of quality. Nations with low birth rates are periodically chastened by more fertile groups. If population exceeds food supply, Nature restores balance through famine, pestilence, and war.

These three laws are the foundation from which the Durants derive everything else: the tension between freedom and equality, the concentration of wealth, and the persistence of war.
