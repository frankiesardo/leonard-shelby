<summary>What is the "comedian test" for product distinctiveness, and why did Ogilvy tell Jaguar to keep the console light switch?</summary>

If a stand-up comedian could do a routine about your product, you're onto something. Idiosyncrasies create "a splinter on the attention" — something slightly off from the normal shape that people notice and remember. Jaguar had a ceiling light controlled by a switch on the center console instead of overhead. They planned to eliminate it for consistency. Ogilvy said keep it: that's your brand. The engineering instinct to smooth out inconsistencies is often right for functionality and often wrong for memorability. Distinctiveness lives in the imperfections.
