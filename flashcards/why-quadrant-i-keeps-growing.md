<summary>You're constantly in crisis mode — fires every day. What does Covey's framework say is the root cause?</summary>

You're living in Quadrant I because you've neglected Quadrant II. Most crises are Quadrant II activities (prevention, planning, relationship-building) that were postponed until they became urgent. The fix isn't better crisis management — it's carving out time for important-but-not-urgent work, even when (especially when) it doesn't feel pressing.

See: [Quadrant II](../ideas/quadrant-ii.md), [P/PC Balance](../ideas/p-pc-balance.md)
