**Q:** What does Lakoff mean by "highlighting and hiding," and what does the TIME IS MONEY metaphor hide?

**A:** Every metaphor simultaneously foregrounds some aspects of a concept and backgrounds others. This is not a flaw -- it is how understanding works. You cannot illuminate everything at once. The danger is forgetting that anything is hidden. TIME IS MONEY highlights that time can be spent, wasted, budgeted, invested, and saved. It hides that time cannot be stored in a bank, cannot be given back, and does not accumulate. It also hides that this way of conceptualizing time is a product of modern industrial culture, not a universal truth -- there are cultures where time is none of these things.
