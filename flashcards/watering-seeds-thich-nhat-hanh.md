<summary>Thich Nhat Hanh taught that "how you live depends on which seeds you water." What does this mean practically, and what technique does Kornfield teach to apply it?</summary>

In the heart and mind there are all kinds of seeds: seeds of joy, love, creativity, peace -- and seeds of fear, anxiety, anger, grasping. You don't need to uproot the unwholesome seeds. You just stop feeding them and deliberately tend the ones you want to grow.

Kornfield's practical technique is the "Top 10 Tunes": number your recurring thought patterns. "Number one: what's happening with my daughter. Number two: the stock portfolio. Number three: the creative project." When a pattern arises in meditation or daily life, you say "oh, number three, thank you" and let it pass. The numbering creates enough distance to recognize the thought without being swallowed by it.

The insight: 90% of your thoughts are repeats. You're running the same unconscious playlist day after day. Making it conscious is the first step. Choosing which tracks to follow is the second. This is not suppression -- it's discernment about where to place your attention.
