<summary>You're building V1 of a product in a market that doesn't exist yet. A team member insists you need more consumer panel data before committing to a direction. What's wrong with this approach, and what should you do instead?</summary>

When creating something genuinely new, there is no meaningful data because nobody has made this before. Consumer panels for unprecedented products tend to give contradictory, useless results -- at Philips, Monday panels chose option X and Friday panels chose option Y. Trying to turn an opinion-driven decision into a data-driven one leads to analysis paralysis.

Instead: use whatever data exists, gather customer insights about their pain points, then follow your gut. Explain your reasoning to the team, take their input, and make the call. For V1, the order is vision first, customer insights second, data third. For V2, the order inverts.

See: [Opinion-Driven Decisions](../ideas/opinion-driven-decisions.md)
