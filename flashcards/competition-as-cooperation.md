<summary>The surfer waits for the big wave. Why? And what does this reveal about the true nature of competition?</summary>

The surfer waits because the big wave draws from him his greatest effort. The obstacles -- the size, the power, the danger -- are what force him to discover the true limits of his capacities. Without them, his potential remains hidden.

This means your opponent is your *friend* to the extent that he makes things difficult for you. True competition is identical with true cooperation: each player tries hardest to defeat the other, but what's being overcome isn't the other person -- it's the obstacles they present. Both players grow stronger.

The practical shift: instead of hoping your opponent double-faults, you want him to get his first serve in -- because that sharpens your return and draws out your best game. The real nice guy is the fierce competitor.
