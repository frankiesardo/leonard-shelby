<summary>What's the difference between a "hostage" customer and a loyal customer, and why does it matter?</summary>

A hostage stays because leaving is too dangerous — the switching cost is high enough that escape is career-ending. A loyal customer stays because the product is genuinely better than alternatives and they would stay even if switching were easy.

The distinction matters because hostage-based moats are fragile to any technology that reduces switching costs (like coding agents cutting SAP-to-Oracle migration complexity), while loyalty-based moats compound over time. Confusing captivity for loyalty leads to underinvestment in product quality.
