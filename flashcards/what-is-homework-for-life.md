<summary>What is "Homework for Life" and why does Matthew Dicks say time doesn't fly?</summary>

Every night, record one moment from the day worth telling as a story — just one line in a spreadsheet. Time doesn't fly; it goes by unaccounted. If you can only remember 89 of 365 days, of course it feels fast. The practice trains your lens for noticing — Dicks went from finding 1.8 moments per day to 7.6 over 12 years.
