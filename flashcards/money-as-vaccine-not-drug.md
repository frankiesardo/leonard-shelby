<summary>What does Morgan Housel mean by "money is a vaccine, not a performance-enhancing drug," and what's the practical implication for how to think about wealth?</summary>

More money tends to reduce bad days but doesn't increase great ones. It prevents misery — like a vaccine prevents disease — but you don't feel daily gratitude for the absence of polio. The drug illusion is that money will make you feel *on fire*, elevated, happy. For brief moments it can, since happiness is fleeting. But it doesn't sustain those moments. The practical implication: stop optimizing money as a happiness machine. Calibrate expectations to "fewer bad days" and orient toward **contentment** instead — a stable "I'm good right here" that doesn't require accumulating more.
