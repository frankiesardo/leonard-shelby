<summary>When Western meditation teachers asked the Dalai Lama about self-hatred, he was confused. Why? And what does Kornfield say is the trap of trying to fix self-judgment?</summary>

The Dalai Lama went back and forth with his translator, then said: "We don't even have a word for that in Tibetan." He was genuinely confused that self-hatred was a widespread experience in the West. Almost every hand in the room went up when he asked how many had experienced it.

The trap: the instinct is to judge your judgment. "Stop that, I hate all that judgment -- how do I get rid of it?" But that's just more judgment. It's the Feedback Loop from Hell applied to the inner critic: feeling bad about feeling bad about yourself.

The alternative is self-compassion, which has three steps (from Kristin Neff and Chris Germer's framework): (1) acknowledge the painful pattern without adding more judgment, (2) recognize common humanity -- this isn't just yours, it's everyone's, (3) bring compassion to yourself and everyone else caught in self-judgment. From this, a confidence grows that isn't brash but quiet -- a sense of self-presence and dignity rather than self-criticism.
