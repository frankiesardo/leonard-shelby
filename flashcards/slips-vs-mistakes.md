<summary>Norman classifies human errors into two categories: slips and mistakes. What is the difference, and why does it matter for design?</summary>

A slip is when the goal is correct but the execution is wrong -- you intended to hit "Save" but hit "Delete" because the buttons look identical. Slips happen at the lower, subconscious levels of the action cycle and are more common in experts (who operate on autopilot). A mistake is when the goal or plan itself is wrong -- you cranked the thermostat to maximum because you believed it was a valve. Mistakes happen at the higher, reflective levels and are more common in novices facing unfamiliar situations. The design responses are different: slips require dissimilar controls, clear feedback, and undo; mistakes require better conceptual models, clearer system state, and root cause analysis. Both require the same underlying attitude: blame the system, not the person.
