<summary>Sony had all the same assets as Apple -- consumer electronics, a music label, content -- yet failed to build an iPod ecosystem. What structural difference between the two companies explains this?</summary>

Sony was organized into autonomous divisions, each with its own P&L, each protecting its own interests. The electronics division worried that a great music player would cannibalize Walkman sales; the music division worried that easy digital distribution would encourage piracy. Apple had one P&L for the entire company and a CEO who forced all teams to work as a single integrated unit. The lesson is that end-to-end integration requires organizational integration -- having the assets is not enough if the structure incentivizes each division to optimize locally rather than globally.
