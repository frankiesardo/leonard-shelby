<summary>Nest included a screwdriver in every thermostat box. Why was this more of a marketing decision than a product decision?</summary>

The screwdriver solved an installation pain point (customers spent 30 minutes hunting for tools), but its real value was as a marketing asset. It lived in kitchen drawers for years, reminding people of Nest every time they opened the drawer. This illustrates a core principle: the product is only a fraction of the customer experience. Every touchpoint -- packaging, installation, support, even a free tool -- is part of the brand. Designing the whole customer journey means treating these "non-product" decisions with the same care as the product itself.

See: [The Whole Customer Journey](../ideas/the-whole-customer-journey.md)
