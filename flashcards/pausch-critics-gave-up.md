<summary>"When you're screwing up and nobody's saying anything to you anymore..." — what does this mean, and why is it the opposite of what most people assume?</summary>

"...that means they gave up." Most people think silence means approval or that they're doing fine. Pausch learned from Coach Graham that the opposite is true: your critics are the ones who still love you and care. When the criticism stops, it's not because you've improved — it's because people have stopped investing in you. Harsh feedback is a sign of commitment; silence is abandonment.
