**Q:** What are the four fundamental strands of Deutsch's "fabric of reality," and why does he argue they must be understood together?

**A:** The four strands are: (1) quantum physics (Everett's many-worlds interpretation), (2) epistemology (Popper's conjectures and refutations), (3) the theory of evolution (Dawkins' selfish gene), and (4) the theory of computation (Turing's universality). Each strand taken individually seems cold, narrow, or incomplete -- physics reduces us to chemical scum, evolution makes us gene vehicles, computation mechanizes the mind, epistemology undermines justified belief. But together they form a non-hierarchical explanatory structure where each fills the gaps in the others. For example, the Turing principle explains *why* knowledge can grow; evolution explains *how*; epistemology explains what distinguishes knowledge from error; quantum physics provides the arena and explains why that arena is comprehensible.
