<summary>Gallwey calls relaxed concentration the "supreme art, because no art can be achieved without it." How do you cultivate it -- and why can't you force it?</summary>

You don't quiet the mind by telling it to shut up. You quiet it by giving it something to focus on. Gallwey's methods: watch the seams of the ball (subtle enough to absorb the mind completely), say "bounce-hit" with the rhythm of the rally, listen to the sound of contact, feel the racket's position through body awareness, and focus on breathing between points.

You cannot force concentration because forcing is Self 1 activity -- it creates tension, not stillness. "Focus is not achieved by staring hard at something." Natural focus occurs when the mind is interested, drawn irresistibly toward the object. The art is creating the conditions for interest, then allowing yourself to fall into focus.

The zone cannot be controlled by Self 1. "Grab for it, and it will squirt away like a slippery bar of soap."
