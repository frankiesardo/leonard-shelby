<summary>Jobs argued that great companies decline when a specific type of person displaces another in leadership. What is the pattern, and why does it happen?</summary>

The pattern: product people build a great company, then sales and marketing people gradually take over because they are the ones who "move the needle on revenues." The salespeople hire more salespeople to run divisions, and the product people stop mattering. The company's output becomes adequate but uninspiring, and decline follows. Jobs saw this at IBM (John Akers), Xerox (executives who ignored PARC), Apple under Sculley, and Microsoft under Ballmer. It happens because a dominant company's growth comes from selling more of what already exists, which privileges sales skills over product innovation -- until a competitor out-innovates them.
