<summary>Why is simplicity the hardest discipline in writing, not the easiest?</summary>

Because simplicity requires clear thinking. You can't strip a sentence to its essentials until you know what you're trying to say — and most people haven't figured that out. The clutter in a sentence is usually a symptom of muddled thought, not lazy editing. Cutting to the bone forces you to confront what you actually mean.
