<summary>What is a Ulysses contract and how does it differ from a forcing function?</summary>

A Ulysses contract is a precommitment device: a decision made by a rational present self to bind a less rational future self. Named for Odysseus tying himself to the mast before the Sirens. Unlike a forcing function (designed into a system by an engineer), a Ulysses contract is self-imposed and temporally structured. It can usually be broken, but breaking it requires a conscious act — that moment of friction is the intervention.
