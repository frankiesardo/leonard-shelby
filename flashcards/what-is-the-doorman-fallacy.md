<summary>What is the "Doorman Fallacy" and what pattern does it reveal about automation decisions?</summary>

A hotel doorman's function gets defined as "opens the door," so consultants replace him with an automatic mechanism. But the doorman was also doing security, hailing taxis, recognizing regulars, and signaling hospitality — none of which appeared on the spreadsheet. The pattern: when you define a role by its most visible function, you systematically miss the tacit, relational value that a human provides. Costs are concrete and immediate; value destruction is diffuse and delayed.
