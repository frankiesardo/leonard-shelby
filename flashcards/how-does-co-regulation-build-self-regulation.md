<summary>How does co-regulation lead to self-regulation? What is the parent's actual job during a child's meltdown?</summary>

Kids borrow the parent's calm through hundreds of repetitions. Each time a parent stays relatively calm while the child is dysregulated, a small amount of regulation capacity transfers to the child's body. Over time, the child accumulates enough stored calm to access it independently. The parent's job during a meltdown is not to stop the meltdown — it's to stay calm. The calm *is* the intervention.
