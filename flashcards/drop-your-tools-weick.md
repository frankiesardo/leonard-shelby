<summary>What is Weick's "dropping your familiar tools" concept, and how did it play out in the Challenger disaster?</summary>

Karl Weick observed that elite firefighters died because they refused to drop their heavy tools when running from advancing fires -- tools defined their identity as firefighters, and dropping them felt like an existential crisis. NASA's Challenger disaster followed the same pattern: engineers could not accept Roger Boisjoly's qualitative, photographic argument that cold temperatures were "away from goodness" because their culture demanded quantitative data. "In God We Trust, All Others Bring Data." Their most reliable tool -- rigorous quantitative analysis -- became the very thing that prevented them from seeing the danger. Seven astronauts died because experienced professionals could not let go of familiar methods in an unfamiliar situation.
