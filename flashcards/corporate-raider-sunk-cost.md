<summary>You're defending past decisions at your company even though they may no longer serve you. What mental exercise can break the sunk cost attachment?</summary>

The Corporate Raider Exercise: imagine the company went bankrupt, you bought it on a fire sale, and you're walking in on day one. Previous management was crazy. What would you change? This creates psychological distance from your own past decisions, letting you evaluate them as an outsider would. Tobi Lütke does this annually, and goes further -- he writes "hit pieces on the past" and publicly trash-talks his own prior work to create permission for everyone to improve without deference.
