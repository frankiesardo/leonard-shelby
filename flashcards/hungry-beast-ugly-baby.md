<summary>What are the "Hungry Beast" and the "Ugly Baby" in Catmull's framework, and why can't you manage them with the same approach?</summary>

The Hungry Beast is the production infrastructure -- the pipeline, schedule, and hundreds of people who need work today. It must be fed constantly or people lose their jobs. The Ugly Baby is the new idea -- formless, unattractive, and vulnerable. Every great film begins as something that looks terrible. The Beast needs efficiency, predictability, and accountability. The Baby needs space, patience, and protection from premature judgment. Organizations that apply pipeline logic to new ideas kill innovation (judging the baby before it can develop). Organizations that apply new-idea logic to the pipeline miss deadlines. Management's central task is balancing these two forces: feed the Beast without letting it devour the babies.
