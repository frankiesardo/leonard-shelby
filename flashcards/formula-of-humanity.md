**Q:** What is Kant's Formula of Humanity, and how does it relate to the three stages of psychological maturity?

**A:** "Act that you use humanity, whether in your own person or in the person of any other, always at the same time as an end, never merely as a means." The child operates on pleasure/pain (everything is a means to feeling good). The adolescent bargains and negotiates (everything is a transaction). The adult acts on unconditional principle (people are ends in themselves). The Formula of Humanity is the bridge from adolescence to adulthood -- the commitment to treat consciousness as inherently valuable, not as a tool.
