<summary>What does Halligan look for in the "C" of his LOCKS algorithm for evaluating founders?</summary>

"C" stands for "Chip on shoulder" — nearly all successful founders have a boulder, not a chip. They carry a deep conviction that the world is wrong in some way and they're going to fix it. This chip drives the obsessive persistence required to survive the inevitable crises of startup life.
