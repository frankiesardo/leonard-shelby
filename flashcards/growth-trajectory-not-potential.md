<summary>Why did Kim Scott and Scott Forstall change the word "potential" to "growth" on Apple's performance management matrix, and what difference did the single word change make?</summary>

"Potential" implies a fixed attribute -- a person either has high potential or doesn't. It's a judgment about who someone is. "Growth trajectory" reframes the same information as a current state that changes over time. A person might be on a steep growth trajectory now (wanting new challenges, ready for more responsibility) and shift to a gradual trajectory later (new parent, recovering from burnout, choosing to go deep). Neither trajectory is inherently better. The word change transformed how managers thought about their people: instead of ranking them ("Is this a high-potential person?"), managers started having conversations ("What trajectory are you on right now? What would help?"). It also eliminated the implicit shame of being "low potential" -- a gradual growth trajectory is a choice, not a deficiency.
