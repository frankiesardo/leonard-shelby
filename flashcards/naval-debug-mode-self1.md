<summary>What does Naval mean by "watch your mind 24/7" and how does it differ from Gallwey's "quiet Self 1"?</summary>

Naval's practice is continuous self-observation: noticing each thought as it arises, asking "does this serve me?", tracing patterns to their origins (childhood conditioning, status anxiety, rehearsal habits). When you see a pattern clearly, it dissolves through understanding, not willpower. This is different from Gallwey's "quiet Self 1" because Self 1 is not silent — it's actively watching. But it's watching without judging or commanding. Naval calls this "meditation 24/7" — not a sit-down activity but a way of relating to your own thoughts all day long.

See: [Self 1 vs Self 2](../ideas/self-1-vs-self-2.md), [Nonjudgmental Awareness](../ideas/nonjudgmental-awareness.md)
