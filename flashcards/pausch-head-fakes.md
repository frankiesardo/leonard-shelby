<summary>What are the two "head fakes" in Pausch's Last Lecture?</summary>

First head fake: the lecture appears to be about achieving childhood dreams, but it's really about how to lead your life. "If you lead your life the right way, the karma will take care of itself. The dreams will come to you."

Second head fake: the talk appears to be for the audience, but it's really for his three kids — who are too young to remember their dying father. Every piece of advice is encoded as a life instruction for children who won't have him there to say it in person.

See: [Meaning Through Suffering](../ideas/meaning-through-suffering.md)
