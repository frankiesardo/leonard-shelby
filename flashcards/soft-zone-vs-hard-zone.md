<summary>You can only focus in perfect silence with no distractions. What's the problem, and what's the alternative?</summary>

That's a "hard zone" — brittle concentration that shatters the moment conditions aren't perfect. The real world is noisy, messy, and full of interruptions. The alternative is the "soft zone": resilient focus that bends with distraction like grass in a hurricane instead of fighting it. Waitzkin trained this by studying chess with music blasting and playing in chaotic park settings. The goal isn't to block distraction but to integrate it — and eventually use it as fuel.
