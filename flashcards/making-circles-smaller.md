<summary>A martial artist knows 50 techniques shallowly. A competitor knows 5 techniques at an invisible level. Who wins in a tournament, and why?</summary>

The competitor with 5 deep techniques wins. Depth beats breadth because deeply internalized technique operates below conscious thought — it's faster, more adaptive, and harder to read. Tyson's knockouts looked impossible because he'd condensed the punch motion until it was barely perceptible while retaining full power. The breadth collector runs out of tricks; the depth specialist has technique that's become instinct.
