<summary>An engineer does exceptional work, loves their current role, and has no interest in becoming a manager. Their boss keeps pushing them toward leadership positions. What concept from Radical Candor explains why this is a mistake, and what should the boss do instead?</summary>

This engineer is a "rock star" -- someone on a gradual growth trajectory who provides exceptional stability and deep expertise. The boss is confusing ambition with value, treating the steep growth trajectory (superstar) as the only valid one. This systematically undervalues rock stars and often drives them out. Instead, the boss should recognize their expertise (make them a guru, give them public presentations, acknowledge their mastery), ensure they have autonomy to go deep, and resist the urge to "develop" them into a role they don't want. Teams need both rock stars and superstars, and the ratio matters.
