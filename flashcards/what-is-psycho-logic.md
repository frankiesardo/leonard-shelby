<summary>What is "psycho-logic" and why does Rory Sutherland say it matters more than economic logic in marketing?</summary>

Psycho-logic is the idea that human behavior follows a different rationality than the one economics assumes. People don't maximize utility; they respond to perceived value, signaling, framing, and context. A 20% price cut may move fewer units than a well-chosen celebrity endorser or a redesigned label — not because consumers are stupid, but because in an uncertain world, psychological signals (trust, status, identity) are rational proxies for quality.

Marketing's job is to operate in this register, not to layer rational arguments on top of product features. "All value is perceived value. There is no other kind."
