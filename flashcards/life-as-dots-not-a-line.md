<summary>Adler says "life is a series of moments, not a line." What is the practical difference between treating life as a line (kinesis) versus a series of dots (energeia)?</summary>

**Life as a line (kinesis):** You are traveling toward a destination -- a career, a legacy, a summit. The present is a "preparatory period." Real life begins later, when you arrive. If you die before reaching the summit, your life was incomplete. Most of your existence is spent "en route."

**Life as a series of dots (energeia):** Each moment is complete in itself, like dancing. The dancing is not a means to get somewhere -- it *is* the point. A life that ends at twenty, lived earnestly, is as complete as a life that ends at ninety.

The practical consequence: stop postponing life. "I'll be happy when I get the promotion" is a life-lie. Living earnestly *now* -- doing what you can, contributing to others -- is itself a complete life. "The greatest life-lie of all is to not live here and now."
