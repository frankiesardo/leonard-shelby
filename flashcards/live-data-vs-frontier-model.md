<summary>Why does a commodity model with live proprietary data beat a frontier model with only public data?</summary>

Live data is continuously updated, context-specific, and deeply tied to the actual workflow — it contains information the frontier model simply doesn't have access to. Health telemetry, transaction streams, operational logs of running systems all provide context richer than any amount of general training.

The key word is "live." Static proprietary datasets are valuable but commoditizing. Live data requires ongoing instrumentation and customer trust — it can't be bought or scraped after the fact, so it compounds as a moat over time rather than eroding.
