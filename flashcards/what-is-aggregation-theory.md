<summary>What is Aggregation Theory and why does it predict that internet-era power concentrates at the demand layer?</summary>

Aggregation Theory (Ben Thompson) says that before the internet, suppliers held power because distribution was scarce. The internet dropped marginal distribution costs to near zero, which shifted power to whoever controls *demand*. Aggregators don't own supply — they own the user relationship. Booking.com is worth more than any hotel chain. Google is worth more than any newspaper. Power concentrates at the top of the aggregation stack, because whoever has the final user relationship can extract rent from everyone beneath them.
