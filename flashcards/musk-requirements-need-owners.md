<summary>Why must every requirement come with a name, not a department?</summary>

You can't challenge a department. A department has no ego to defend, no memory, no accountability. Requirements attached to departments survive long after their originators leave and their justification evaporates.

A named person can be asked to defend the requirement, must take responsibility for it, and will update or retract it when evidence changes. Without a name, a requirement is effectively immortal — it can never be falsified, because there is no one to falsify it with.

See: [Requirements Need Owners](../ideas/requirements-need-owners.md)
