<summary>What are the three measures in throughput accounting and why must throughput be prioritized over the other two?</summary>

The three measures are: (1) **Throughput** -- the rate at which the system generates money through sales, (2) **Inventory** -- all money invested in things the system intends to sell, and (3) **Operating expense** -- all money spent to turn inventory into throughput.

Throughput must be prioritized because it has no theoretical upper limit -- you can always sell more. Inventory and operating expense have a lower bound of zero -- you can only cut so much. A "cost world" mentality focuses on cutting expense, which leads to layoffs, balanced plants, and diminishing returns. A "throughput world" mentality focuses on selling more by removing constraints, which has compounding returns. In Rogo's plant, the cost-accounting mindset celebrated the robots' high efficiency while the plant lost money. Throughput accounting revealed that the robots increased operating expense and inventory without adding a single dollar of throughput.
