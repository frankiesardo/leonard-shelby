<summary>What is Alan Watts's "Backwards Law," and why does conventional self-help often backfire according to this principle?</summary>

The Backwards Law: wanting a positive experience is itself a negative experience; accepting a negative experience is itself a positive experience. Pursuing something reinforces the fact that you lack it.

Conventional self-help backfires because it starts from the premise that you are not enough. Standing in front of a mirror repeating "I am confident" reminds you that you don't feel confident. The pursuit of feeling good all the time makes you feel worse, because every moment you don't feel good becomes evidence of failure.

The fix: instead of chasing positive experiences, accept the negative ones. Instead of asking "how do I feel good?" ask "what struggle am I willing to sustain?"
