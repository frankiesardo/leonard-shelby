<summary>According to Sutherland, why can't publicly traded companies do marketing well (with a few exceptions like P&G and Unilever)?</summary>

PLCs are "incentivized to behave like psychopaths" — they optimize for short-term transactional value, not long-term relationship building. The requirement for quarterly self-justification and numbers overrides the real purpose of marketing, which is investment in long-term customer value. Private and family-owned companies (Dyson, Costco, Aldi) can invest in customer relationships because they're not controlled by the finance function. Buffett's directive captures the difference: "Treat this company as if you and your family have 100% of your money in it for 100 years."
