<summary>Why did Jobs say getting fired from Apple was the best thing that ever happened to him?</summary>

"The heaviness of being successful was replaced by the lightness of being a beginner again, less sure about everything." Being fired freed him from the burden of maintaining his position and reputation. In the five years that followed, he started NeXT (whose technology became the foundation of Apple's renaissance), bought Pixar (which created *Toy Story*), and met his wife. None of it would have happened without the failure. The key: he still loved what he did — the rejection didn't change that.
