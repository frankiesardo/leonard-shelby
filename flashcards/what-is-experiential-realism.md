<summary>What is experiential realism, and how does it differ from both objectivism and relativism?</summary>

Experiential realism is Lakoff's third path between objectivism and relativism. It shares objectivism's commitments: a real world exists, stable knowledge is possible, truth goes beyond mere coherence. But it grounds meaning in **embodied understanding** rather than in symbol-to-world correspondence.

**vs. Objectivism**: Objectivism says truth is determined by how symbols correspond to a mind-independent world. Lakoff argues (via Putnam's theorem) that such correspondence can't fix reference — there are always multiple valid interpretations. Meaning requires a knower with a body.

**vs. Relativism**: Experiential realism is not "anything goes." Embodiment *constrains* what concepts are possible. Shared bodies produce shared understanding. Different cultures may conceptualize differently, but all are constrained by universal physics and universal physiology.

Truth is real but always relative to a system of understanding — and systems of understanding are grounded in how bodies interact with environments.
