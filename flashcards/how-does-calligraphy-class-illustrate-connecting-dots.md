<summary>How does Jobs's calligraphy class illustrate the principle that you can't connect the dots looking forward?</summary>

After dropping out of Reed College, Jobs dropped in on a calligraphy class purely out of curiosity — it had no practical application. Ten years later, when designing the Macintosh, it all came back: the Mac became the first computer with beautiful typography. Since Windows copied the Mac, every personal computer inherited that decision. The lesson: following curiosity into apparent dead ends can produce outsized returns, but only in retrospect.
