<summary>How do Zinsser's "think small" and Waitzkin's "making smaller circles" express the same principle?</summary>

Both argue that depth into a narrow area reveals more than breadth across a wide one. Zinsser says to reduce your subject until it's human-scale — one corner, one episode, one detail — because a precisely drawn fragment illuminates the whole. Waitzkin says to condense a technique to its essence rather than collecting more techniques, because the principles driving one small motion fuel the entire system. In writing and in martial arts, the path to the macro runs through the micro.
