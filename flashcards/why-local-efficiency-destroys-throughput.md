<summary>Why does running every machine at maximum efficiency actually hurt a manufacturing plant's performance?</summary>

Because running a non-bottleneck at full speed when the bottleneck can't absorb its output doesn't increase throughput -- it only increases inventory. The excess parts pile up, carrying costs climb, and the plant's cash flow deteriorates. In Rogo's plant, the robots ran at 90%+ efficiency while the plant lost money. The parts they produced sat in mountains of work-in-process because the bottleneck couldn't process them fast enough. Activating a resource is not the same as utilizing it. Utilization means the work contributes to throughput. Everything else is waste disguised as productivity.
