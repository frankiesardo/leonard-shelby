<summary>Why does Lakoff insist that prototype effects are NOT the same as category structure?</summary>

Because goodness-of-example ratings reflect how well something fits an idealized cognitive model, not degrees of membership. A penguin is 100% a bird but a poor example because it doesn't fly, sing, or perch — it doesn't fit the bird ICM well. The proof: even classically defined categories like "odd number" produce prototype effects (3 seems like a "better" odd number than 447), because our *understanding* of numbers involves privileged models (single digits, multiples of 10), even though all odd numbers are equally odd.

The conflation of effects with structure — the "EFFECTS = STRUCTURE interpretation" — is the error that led critics to wrongly claim prototype theory was refuted.
