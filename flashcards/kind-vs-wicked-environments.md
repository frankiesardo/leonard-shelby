<summary>What's the difference between kind and wicked learning environments, and why does it matter for how we train people?</summary>

In kind environments (chess, golf), patterns repeat, rules are clear, and feedback is immediate -- deliberate practice works and early specialization pays off. In wicked environments (management, medicine, entrepreneurship), rules are unclear, patterns don't repeat, and feedback is delayed or misleading -- experience can teach the wrong lessons. Most of real life is wicked, which means the Tiger Woods path of early specialization is the exception, not the rule. Breadth, analogical thinking, and late specialization are the better strategies for wicked domains.
