<summary>Why does positive visualization alone impede goal achievement, and what's the fix?</summary>

Gabriele Oettingen's research shows that people who only imagine success are less likely to achieve it — visualization creates a false sense of completion that reduces effort. The fix is "mental contrasting": imagine success (backcasting) AND imagine failure (premortem). Together they map both the path to the goal and the landmines along the way. Prospective hindsight increases correct identification of future outcomes by roughly 30%.
