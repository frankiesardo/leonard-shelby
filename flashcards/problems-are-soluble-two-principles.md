<summary>What are the two companion principles of Deutsch's optimism, and why must they always go together?</summary>

The two principles are:

1. **Problems are soluble.** Every evil is caused by a lack of knowledge, and there is no law of nature preventing us from acquiring the relevant knowledge.
2. **Problems are inevitable.** Every solution creates new problems. There is no final state, no equilibrium, no sustainable endpoint.

They must go together because either one alone is misleading. "Problems are soluble" without "problems are inevitable" becomes utopianism -- the false promise of a perfected state. "Problems are inevitable" without "problems are soluble" becomes nihilism -- the belief that struggle is pointless.

Together they describe the only sustainable mode of existence: perpetual problem-solving, moving from problems to *better problems*, never arriving but always progressing.
