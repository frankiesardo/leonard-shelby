<summary>Why does Epstein argue that match quality matters more than grit, and what is the optimal strategy for finding it?</summary>

Match quality -- the fit between a person and their work -- is more predictive of success than raw persistence. Grit without fit is wasted effort. Van Gogh had terrible grit by Duckworth's measure (40th percentile), but his relentless testing of different careers and styles led him to the unique fit that changed art. The optimal strategy is the "multi-armed bandit": sample broadly, gather information, and gradually shift toward the best match. Short-term planning beats long-term planning. "Here's who I am at the moment, here are my motivations, here's what I'd like to learn -- which of these opportunities is the best match right now?"
