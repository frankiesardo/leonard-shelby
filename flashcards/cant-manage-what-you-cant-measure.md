<summary>Why does Catmull call the maxim "you can't manage what you can't measure" ridiculous?</summary>

Because it's "something said by people who are unaware of how much is hidden." A large portion of what matters in any organization cannot be measured -- morale, creative momentum, the quality of interpersonal trust, the ideas people aren't sharing. When leaders treat data as a complete picture, they ignore what they can't see, and their blind spots become structural. Catmull's alternative: measure what you can, evaluate what you measure, and appreciate that you cannot measure the vast majority of what you do. Data is one lens, not the answer. The danger isn't lack of data -- it's the illusion that data paints a full picture.
