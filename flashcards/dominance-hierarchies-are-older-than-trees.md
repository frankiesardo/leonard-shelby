<summary>What does the lobster serotonin system demonstrate about the nature of social hierarchies?</summary>

Lobsters and humans share serotonin-based neural circuitry that governs posture, confidence, and willingness to compete -- circuitry that is approximately 350 million years old (older than trees). Prozac literally works on lobsters. This demonstrates that dominance hierarchies are not a recent cultural invention but a biological inheritance embedded in the oldest parts of the nervous system. Any serious attempt to address inequality must reckon with this constraint rather than treating hierarchy as purely a product of culture or malice.
