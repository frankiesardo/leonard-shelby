<summary>Why did Jobs's "Reality Distortion Field" sometimes produce extraordinary results and sometimes produce spectacular failures -- and what distinguished the two outcomes?</summary>

The RDF worked when Jobs's insistence bent *conventional wisdom* -- deadlines that seemed impossible because nobody had tried hard enough, design constraints that engineers accepted out of habit rather than physics. It failed when it bent *actual reality* -- the NeXT Computer's pricing, the Lisa's complexity, the initial denial of his cancer diagnosis. The distinguishing factor was whether the "impossibility" was a social agreement (breakable) or a material constraint (not breakable). The people who thrived under the RDF were those who could tell the difference and channel the pressure into creative solutions rather than false promises.
