<summary>What is the Sawyer Effect, and why do contingent rewards reduce intrinsic motivation?</summary>

The Sawyer Effect is the phenomenon where extrinsic rewards transform an interesting task into a drudge -- turning play into work. Contingent, expected, if-then rewards ("If you do X, you'll get Y") signal that the task is undesirable, shift the person's sense of control from internal to external, and make the reward the reason for the activity. Remove the reward and the reason vanishes -- along with the interest that preceded it. Deci's Soma puzzle study showed that people paid to solve puzzles, then unpaid, played *less* than people who were never paid.
