<summary>What are Lakoff and Johnson's three types of conceptual metaphor, and how do they build on each other?</summary>

(1) **Orientational metaphors** map spatial experience onto abstract domains: HAPPY IS UP, SAD IS DOWN, MORE IS UP, CONTROL IS UP. They are grounded directly in bodily experience (drooping posture = sadness, adding substance = level rises). (2) **Ontological metaphors** let us treat abstract experiences as entities and substances: inflation as a thing we can "combat," the mind as a machine that "breaks down." They build on our experience of bounded physical objects. (3) **Structural metaphors** map the full rich structure of one domain onto another: ARGUMENT IS WAR, TIME IS MONEY, LIFE IS A JOURNEY. They build on both orientational and ontological metaphors and provide the most detailed understanding. Together they form a layered system grounded in bodily experience.
