<summary>How did "user imagery" kill the Ford/Edison electric car in the early 1900s, and what modern product faces the same problem?</summary>

The electric car was quiet, required no hand-cranking, and produced no fumes — all attributes that made it popular with women. It became stereotyped as a "woman's car" while farm boys defined real cars through gasoline engine racing. User imagery killed it for a century. Today's EVs face a similar problem: non-owners see EV drivers as "smug environmental tossers," even though most buyers are car/tech enthusiasts, not environmentalists. People don't just evaluate the product — they evaluate whether they want to be the kind of person who uses it.
