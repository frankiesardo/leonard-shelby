<summary>What is Musk's calibration test for whether you're deleting aggressively enough?</summary>

If you're not adding back about 10% of what you deleted, you're not deleting enough.

The logic: deletion should be aggressive enough that you occasionally overshoot and have to restore something. If you never restore anything, your deletions were too timid — you only cut what was obviously useless. The real waste hides in things that *seem* necessary until tested against reality.
