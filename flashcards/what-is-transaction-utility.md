<summary>In Thaler's "beer on the beach" experiment, why does willingness to pay change when the beer comes from a hotel vs. a shack — even though the beer is identical?</summary>

This is transaction utility: people derive pleasure or pain not just from the good itself, but from the perceived quality of the deal. We factor in the seller's imagined overheads, our sense of fairness, and the social meaning of the exchange. The actual utility is identical; the felt utility is not. This means presentation, environment, and human interaction around a sale are not decorative extras — they are part of the product's experienced value.

See: [Transaction Utility](../ideas/transaction-utility.md)

</details>
