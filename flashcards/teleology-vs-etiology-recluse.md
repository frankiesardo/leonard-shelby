<summary>A man has shut himself in his room for years. Freud would look for a childhood trauma that caused this. How does Adler's teleological view explain the same situation differently?</summary>

Adler reverses the causation. The man is not shut in *because* of trauma. He has the **goal** of not going out, and he manufactures anxiety and fear as tools to achieve that goal. Staying home gets him parental attention, avoids social judgment, and preserves a known identity.

The key Adlerian claim: "No experience is in itself a cause of our success or failure. We do not suffer from the shock of our experiences -- the so-called trauma -- but instead we make out of them whatever suits our purposes."

Etiology (study of causes) leads to determinism: the past controls you. Teleology (study of purposes) leads to freedom: you can choose different goals.

See: [Teleology vs. Etiology](../ideas/teleology-vs-etiology.md)

</details>
