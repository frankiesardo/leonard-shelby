<summary>Pausch says "brick walls are there for a reason." What reason — and how does this connect to Gallwey's "competition as cooperation"?</summary>

Brick walls are not there to keep you out. They're there to give you a chance to show how badly you want something — because they stop the people who don't want it badly enough. The wall is a filter that works in your favor. This is the same insight as Gallwey's surfer: the big wave (the obstacle, the rival, the wall) is what draws out your greatest effort. Without resistance, you never discover what you're capable of. The obstacle is the gift.
