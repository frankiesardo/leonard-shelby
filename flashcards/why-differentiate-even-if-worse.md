<summary>Why would you deliberately build something worse than what already exists in the market?</summary>

A copied seven-out-of-ten has a ceiling -- you can never iterate past the original because you don't understand the decisions behind it. A home-built six-out-of-ten has a trajectory -- you own every decision and can iterate indefinitely, eventually passing the seven. The world belongs to the fast: the people who iterate, adjust, and prune. You can only prune what you planted.

See: [Differentiate Even If Worse](../ideas/differentiate-even-if-worse.md)
