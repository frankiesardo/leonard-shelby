<summary>In the University of Rochester study, what happened to graduates who achieved their "profit goals" versus those who achieved their "purpose goals"?</summary>

Graduates with **purpose goals** (helping others, learning) who felt they were attaining them reported higher satisfaction, well-being, and low anxiety and depression. Graduates with **profit goals** (wealth, fame) who achieved them were *no happier* than in college -- and showed *increases* in anxiety and depression. Even when you get what you want, it's not always what you need. Attaining the wrong goals makes you worse off, not better. This is Pink's capstone argument for why Motivation 3.0 (autonomy, mastery, purpose) must replace the profit-maximizing operating system.
