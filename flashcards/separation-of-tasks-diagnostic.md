<summary>A parent is agonizing over their teenager's refusal to study. According to Adler, what single question resolves this, and what does the answer imply?</summary>

Ask: **"Who ultimately bears the consequences of this choice?"** The child receives the grades, faces the consequences of ignorance, and lives with the results. Therefore, studying is the child's task.

The parent can inform, express concern, and offer assistance -- but cannot intrude. Forcing the child to study is a task-boundary violation. It produces rebellion or dependence, not learning. "You can lead a horse to water, but you can't make him drink."

This applies to every interpersonal conflict: most suffering comes from intruding on other people's tasks, or allowing others to intrude on yours.

See: [Separation of Tasks](../ideas/separation-of-tasks.md)
