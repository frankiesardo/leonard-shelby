<summary>Gallwey compliments a group of women on their footwork, and their performance immediately drops. Why are positive judgments just as harmful as negative ones?</summary>

A compliment establishes a standard. The ego-mind reasons: "If he's pleased with good performance, he'll be displeased with bad performance." Now instead of simply observing their feet (which was producing great results), they're trying to live up to an expectation. Self 1 takes over, and the natural ease disappears.

Positive and negative evaluations are relative to each other -- you can't have one without the other. Both activate the judgment cycle: evaluate, analyze, self-instruct, try hard, tighten up, evaluate again. The alternative isn't denial (you still see the ball went out) -- it's description without emotional charge. Observe like an umpire: call the ball, don't frown or smile.
