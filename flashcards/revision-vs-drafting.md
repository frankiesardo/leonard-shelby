<summary>How does "revision is composition" differ from "write a rough draft, then revise"?</summary>

The draft-then-revise model treats creation and refinement as separate phases, which devalues the most generative part of the work. "Revision is composition" collapses them into one act: bring each sentence as close to final as you can before moving to the next. No provisional sentences. This sounds slower but avoids the tyranny of what already exists — once mediocre words are on the page, they acquire inertia that makes them hard to discard.
