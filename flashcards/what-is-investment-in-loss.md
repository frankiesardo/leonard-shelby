<summary>Why is getting thrown around by a stronger opponent a better learning strategy than drilling against weaker ones?</summary>

Because ego-protection is the enemy of growth. Training against weaker opponents lets you feel competent but teaches nothing new. Getting thrown around by a stronger opponent forces you past your current limits — your body learns to absorb impact, your mind learns to read attacks, and eventually you begin to sense openings. The discomfort is the signal that learning is happening. Michael Jordan missed more last-second shots than anyone in NBA history. That wasn't a footnote to his greatness — it was the mechanism.
