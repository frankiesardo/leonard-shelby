<summary>Nine out of ten heroin-addicted soldiers quit after Vietnam. Why did the typical rehab patient relapse at 90%?</summary>

The soldiers returned to a radically different environment -- no cues, no triggers, no social context that prompted heroin use. Their addiction dissolved because the environment that sustained it was gone. Rehab patients return to the same neighborhood, the same friends, the same apartment -- the same cues that created the habit in the first place. Self-control is a short-term strategy; in the long run, we become a product of the environment we live in. Design the environment, don't rely on willpower.
