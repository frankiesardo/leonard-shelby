<summary>Why does Thompson argue AI ads should be Meta-style rather than Google-style, and what's wrong with context-matched ads in AI conversations?</summary>

Context-matched ads (showing ads relevant to the query) have three problems: (1) the ad inventory depends on conversations matching available ads, shrinking the market; (2) showing an ad related to the answer raises user suspicion about whether the answer itself was influenced; (3) the query context is a weak signal compared to a full user profile. The better model: build a deep profile of the user across all interactions and serve ads relevant to *who they are*, not *what they asked* — exactly what Meta does. Google's ideal play is never running ads inside Gemini at all, but using Gemini to improve targeting across YouTube and Search.
