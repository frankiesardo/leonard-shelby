<summary>How do you distinguish a rational meme from an anti-rational meme in practice?</summary>

**Rational memes** replicate by relying on their holders' critical faculties. They survive because they are true, useful, or beautiful, and they outcompete rivals under scrutiny. Example: Newton's laws spread because they worked for diverse purposes -- cathedrals, bridges, artillery.

**Anti-rational memes** replicate by *disabling* their holders' critical faculties. They survive through fear, conformity pressure, or psychological manipulation. Example: religious taboos that instill terror of supernatural punishment for questioning.

Deutsch's diagnostic: whenever you find yourself enacting **complex, narrowly defined behavior** that has been accurately repeated from one holder to the next, be suspicious. If the behavior **thwarts your personal objectives**, if it **persists when its ostensible justification disappears**, and especially if your own explanations for it are **bad explanations** -- those are signs of an anti-rational meme. "Because I say so" and "it never did me any harm" are its signatures.
