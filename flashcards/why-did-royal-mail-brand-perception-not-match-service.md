<summary>Royal Mail invested heavily in service quality but found brand perception didn't correlate with service levels at all. What actually drove customer perception?</summary>

Whether you liked your postman. Areas with unreliable service but a friendly postman who did favors, left things in the porch, and had a chat — those people thought Royal Mail was brilliant. The explanation: we have a quarter-million years of evolved experience judging people and essentially zero evolved experience evaluating postal logistics. We use our assessment of the human as a proxy for the quality of the institution. This isn't irrational — it's a reliable heuristic for downside variance reduction.
