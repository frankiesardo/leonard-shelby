<summary>What is the single most important design constraint of Pixar's Braintrust, and why does removing it break the mechanism?</summary>

The Braintrust has no authority over the director. Members diagnose problems and offer perspectives, but the director is free to ignore every note. This is the entire design. If you give the feedback group power over the project, two things happen: the director stops listening honestly (they're now managing their boss, not processing information), and the feedback group starts hedging (they'll be held responsible if their notes don't work). Removing authority allows both sides to be candid.
