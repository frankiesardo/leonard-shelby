<summary>What are "factory settings" in the context of attachment theory, and why do they persist into adulthood?</summary>

Factory settings are the defaults our earliest relationships install about who we are, what feelings are safe, and what we can expect from people. They persist because the body generalizes early lessons aggressively — the same way you look both ways before crossing the street in a city you've never visited. A child who learned "you're overreacting" runs the same circuit decades later when deciding whether to trust their gut in a completely different situation.
