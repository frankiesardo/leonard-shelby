<summary>Why does Manson say "What pain do you want to sustain?" is a more useful question than "What do you want out of life?"</summary>

Everyone wants the same things: happiness, love, success. The question "What do you want?" is so common it's meaningless. What differentiates people is not what they want but what they're willing to struggle for.

Manson spent years fantasizing about being a rock star — but he didn't want the daily drudgery of practice, the logistics, the gear-hauling, the rejection. He loved the summit but hated the climb. So it never happened.

The people who actually achieve things are the ones who enjoy (or at least accept) the struggle: the gym-lover gets the physique, the corporate-politics-lover gets the corner office, the rejection-tolerant artist gets the career. You are defined by what you're willing to struggle for, not by what you dream about.
