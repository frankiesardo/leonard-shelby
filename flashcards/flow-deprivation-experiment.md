<summary>What happened when Csikszentmihalyi asked people to eliminate flow-producing activities from their lives for 48 hours?</summary>

After just two days without flow, participants showed symptoms resembling generalized anxiety disorder: restlessness, difficulty concentrating, irritability, fatigue, muscle tension, and sleep disturbance. The deterioration in mood was so advanced that prolonging the experiment would have been unadvisable. This demonstrates that flow -- the state produced by Goldilocks tasks (challenges perfectly matched to ability) -- is not a luxury. It is, as Pink puts it, "the oxygen of the soul."
