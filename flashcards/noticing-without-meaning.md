<summary>You notice something interesting. Klinkenborg says to assign it no meaning. Why?</summary>

Because the moment you rush to categorize or exploit a perception, you narrow the aperture of your awareness. Noticing is upstream of meaning-making. If you immediately convert what you see into "material" or a conclusion, you stop seeing. The discipline is to stay open — notice what you notice, let it pass, and trust that your mind retains what matters. The meaning will emerge later, on its own terms.
