<summary>In the happiness formula H = S + C + V, why does V (voluntary activities) offer more leverage than C (conditions), and what are the most effective V activities?</summary>

Conditions (C) are constant over time -- a new house, a higher salary, a better climate -- so they are subject to the hedonic treadmill. You adapt to them, and they fade from awareness. Voluntary activities (V) require ongoing choice, effort, and attention, so they cannot disappear from consciousness. They resist adaptation by their very nature.

The most effective V activities from research:
- **Gratitude exercises** -- counting blessings or writing gratitude letters produces sustained mood improvement
- **Acts of kindness** -- even one per week produces small but lasting happiness gains
- **Using your character strengths** -- activities matched to your top strengths (curiosity, kindness, zest, etc.) are intrinsically rewarding
- **Pursuing flow** -- activities that challenge your skills with immediate feedback
- **Meditation** -- trains the elephant to be less reactive to ups and downs

The Buddha and Stoics focused on V (cultivate detachment, practice acceptance). Haidt agrees V matters most but argues they were wrong to dismiss C entirely: some conditions -- especially relationships and control over stressors -- genuinely resist adaptation and are worth striving for.
