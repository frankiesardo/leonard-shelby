<summary>How does Alexander's rule 10 — "learn the rules first, then break them" — connect to Waitzkin's "numbers to leave numbers"?</summary>

They describe the same arc. Beginners start half-baked but sometimes talented. Experts teach them The Right Way, and they become stilted and formulaic. Eventually they master the rules so thoroughly that the rules dissolve into instinct — and then they break them freely and it works. This is Waitzkin's "form to leave form": conscious scaffolding internalized until it becomes unconscious. Orwell's version: "Break any of these rules rather than say something outright barbarous." The writer who has never learned the rules breaks them clumsily. The writer who has internalized them breaks them with precision.
