**Q:** What are the four causes of human violence according to Storr, and which two actually dominate?

**A:** The four causes are: (1) greed and ambition, (2) sadism, (3) high self-esteem, and (4) moral idealism. Popular belief and cliched stories emphasize greed and sadism, but these are vanishingly rare. It is actually high self-esteem and moral idealism -- convictions of personal and moral superiority -- that drive most acts of evil. The brain's hero-maker generates self-justifying narratives so effectively that even murderers consider themselves above average in kindness and morality. This is why great drama forms around a clash of competing hero-maker narratives, not a simple good-vs-evil binary.
