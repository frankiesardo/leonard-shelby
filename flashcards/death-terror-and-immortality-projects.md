<summary>What are Becker's "immortality projects," and what is his "bitter antidote" for the problems they create?</summary>

**Immortality projects** are the ways humans try to make their conceptual self outlive their physical self: building institutions, creating art, having children, pursuing fame, founding religions. According to Ernest Becker (*The Denial of Death*), all of human civilization is essentially a collection of immortality projects driven by "death terror" — the uniquely human awareness of our own inevitable death.

The **problem**: when immortality projects become the primary motivation, they breed dogmatism, conflict, and fragility. Wars erupt when one group's immortality project collides with another's. Personal crises occur when your project fails and the death terror floods back in.

The **bitter antidote**: instead of building ever-grander immortality projects, accept the inevitability of death. Sit with the terror. When you become comfortable with the root fear, you choose your values more freely — unrestrained by the desperate quest for permanence and freed from dangerous dogmatic views.
