<summary>How does Peterson connect individual dishonesty to political totalitarianism?</summary>

Following Solzhenitsyn, Peterson argues that individual lies aggregate into systemic lies. Each time a person falsifies their speech or avoids a necessary truth for comfort, they weaken their own character and contribute to a culture of deception. A society of people who routinely shade the truth is a society that will accept and sustain totalitarian lies -- because no one has the moral backbone to resist. The gulag was built "one small cowardice at a time." The antidote is not heroic truth-telling but the simpler discipline of refusing to say things you know to be false.
