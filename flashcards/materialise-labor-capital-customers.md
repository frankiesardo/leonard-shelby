<summary>What are the three things Alex Rampell looks for in founders, and why does the "Count of Monte Cristo" motivation matter?</summary>

Rampell looks for founders who can materialise labor (convince people to follow them for a pay cut), capital (tell a compelling enough story to raise), and customers (land sales with zero track record). The Count of Monte Cristo motivation — revenge, redemption, a chip on the shoulder — matters because at some point someone will offer the founder life-changing money. Only founders driven by something beyond money will turn it down and keep building.
