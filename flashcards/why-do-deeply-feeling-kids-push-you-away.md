<summary>Why do deeply feeling kids push you away in the exact moments they need you most? What's the side-door alternative?</summary>

For DFKs, vulnerability sits right next to shame. They're acutely aware that their reactions are bigger than other kids', and they fear their intensity will overwhelm the adults they depend on. Pushing you away is a preemptive strike against the disconnection they expect. Front-door approaches ("I see you're upset") activate the shame and make things worse. Side-door strategies go around the shame: the thumbs up/down game, leading with something absurd they can reject first, being physically present without trying to fix anything.

See: [Deeply Feeling Kids](../ideas/deeply-feeling-kids.md)
