<summary>Ajahn Chah told Kornfield there are "two kinds of suffering." What are they, and which one is the gateway to freedom?</summary>

The suffering you run away from follows you everywhere. The suffering you face is the gateway to freedom.

Kornfield's teacher said this as a greeting when Kornfield arrived at the forest monastery: "I hope you're not afraid to suffer." Then the distinction: running from suffering perpetuates it; facing suffering transforms it. This maps onto Frankl's claim that unavoidable suffering, when faced with the right attitude, can become a source of meaning.

The practical implication: the meditation training Kornfield received was precisely the art of facing suffering -- sitting with anger, grief, fear -- rather than running from it through distraction, suppression, or reaction.
