<summary>What is the origin of "Stay Hungry, Stay Foolish" and what does it mean?</summary>

It appeared on the back cover of the final issue of Stewart Brand's *Whole Earth Catalog* in the mid-1970s, beneath a photograph of an early morning country road. It was Brand's farewell message. Jobs encountered it as a young man and adopted it as a personal mantra.

"Hungry" means never arriving — maintaining appetite and refusing to be satisfied with what you already know. "Foolish" means willingness to look stupid — following your intuition before you have credentials, permission, or certainty. Together: stay unsatisfied enough to keep moving, and stay naive enough to try things experts would dismiss.
