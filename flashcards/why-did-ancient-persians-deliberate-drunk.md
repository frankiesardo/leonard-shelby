<summary>According to Herodotus, the ancient Persians debated everything twice — once sober and once drunk. What asymmetry in modern business does this illuminate?</summary>

They only proceeded if a decision passed both the rational test (sober) and the emotional/creative test (drunk). In modern business, there's a fundamental asymmetry: creative people always have to present ideas to rational people for approval. But rational people never present their conclusions to creative people for a sanity check. This means psychological and creative solutions get killed before they're born — they pass the "drunk" test but never survive the "sober" committee. Having a two-stage lock (rational + emotional) would produce better decisions than either mode alone.
