<summary>Why does Peterson argue that meaning is found at the border between order and chaos, rather than safely inside order?</summary>

Too much order produces tyranny and stagnation -- everything is predictable but nothing grows. Too much chaos produces dissolution and terror -- everything is possible but nothing is stable. Meaning emerges at the boundary where you are grounded enough to function but exposed enough to encounter the unknown and learn from it. The Taoist yin-yang symbol captures this: each pole contains the seed of the other, and the productive life oscillates between them.
