<summary>Velasco's hitter spikes out of bounds. What's the difference between "the set was bad" and "the ball is low" — and why does it determine the outcome?</summary>

"The set was bad" is judgment — it opens the complaint file, assigns blame to the setter, and wastes the fraction of a second the hitter needs to act. "The ball is low" is observation — it opens the solution file ("what are my options for a low ball?"). The brain is an extraordinary computer with files for every scenario, but only if you give it the right query. Judgment gives it the wrong query. This is Gallwey's nonjudgmental awareness applied to volleyball: perception without evaluation creates space for action.
