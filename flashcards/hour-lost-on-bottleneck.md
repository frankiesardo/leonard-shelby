<summary>Why is an hour lost on a bottleneck an hour lost for the entire system, while an hour saved on a non-bottleneck is worthless?</summary>

A bottleneck's capacity equals or is less than demand -- it is already the limiting factor. Every hour it runs produces throughput the system can sell. So every hour it sits idle (for lunch breaks, setups on unnecessary jobs, processing defective parts) directly reduces the system's total output by whatever that hour would have produced. There is no way to recover that lost hour elsewhere.

A non-bottleneck, by definition, has more capacity than the constraint needs. Saving an hour on it doesn't increase throughput -- the constraint still can't process any faster. The extra hour of non-bottleneck output just becomes inventory. This is why Jonah told Rogo to do quality inspection before the bottleneck (not after) and to stagger worker breaks so the bottleneck never sits idle.
