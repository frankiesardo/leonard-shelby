<summary>Why is frustration tolerance eroding for both kids and parents simultaneously, and why does this create a vicious cycle?</summary>

Technology collapsed the space between wanting and having — for kids (instant dopamine, no effort) and parents (no more Blockbuster, everything delivered). Kids have less tolerance for frustration, so they have more bad behavior. Parents have less tolerance for their kids' bad behavior, so they capitulate faster ("fine, take the iPad"). Capitulation teaches even less tolerance. Both sides are losing the skill simultaneously, each making the other worse.
