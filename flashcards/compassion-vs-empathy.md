<summary>Kornfield distinguishes between empathy and compassion using a schoolyard image. What is the difference?</summary>

A kid is being bullied on the schoolyard. Empathy is when you *feel* for that kid -- your nervous system resonates with their pain, like a violin on a table vibrating in sympathy with a bowed violin nearby. That's the mirror neuron response.

Compassion is what happens next. Compassion *moves* you -- to walk over, to talk to the teacher, to say "there's suffering here, let's do something." Kornfield's formulation: "Compassion is a verb." It is empathy plus the heart's response to alleviate suffering.

The distinction matters because empathy alone can lead to burnout (you absorb others' pain without channeling it). And action without empathy misses the actual need (you fix the problem you imagine rather than the one they have). The complete circuit: feel with (empathy) -> be moved to act (compassion) -> act wisely (discernment).
