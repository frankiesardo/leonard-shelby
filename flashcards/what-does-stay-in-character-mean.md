<summary>What does "stay in character" mean when speaking, and why is breaking character so damaging?</summary>

People can't see what you feel. Breaking character — "sorry, I'm not making sense" — forces a lens of doubt onto your audience that wasn't there. Like a pilot announcing the cockpit warning light: it doesn't help, it makes every bump feel fatal. The cue: stay in it, especially when it's worst. The feedback loop is self-reinforcing — appear confident, feel confident.
