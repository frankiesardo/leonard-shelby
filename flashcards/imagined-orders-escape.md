<summary>Why can you never truly escape an imagined order, according to Harari?</summary>

Because dismantling one imagined order requires believing in another. The revolutionary who overthrows the monarchy believes in the nation-state. The libertarian who rejects government believes in property rights. You cannot run a large-scale society on objective reality alone -- every attempt to break free just lands you in "the more spacious exercise yard of a bigger prison." The question is never whether to have an imagined order, but which one.
