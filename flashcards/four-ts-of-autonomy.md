<summary>What are Pink's four dimensions of autonomy, and what happens when organizations provide them?</summary>

The four T's are: **Task** (what you do), **Time** (when you do it), **Technique** (how you do it), and **Team** (whom you do it with). When organizations provide autonomy across these dimensions, the results are striking: Google's 20% time produced Gmail and Google News. Best Buy's ROWE increased productivity 35% and slashed turnover. Cornell found that businesses offering autonomy grew at four times the rate of control-oriented firms with one-third the turnover. Autonomy is not independence -- it's acting with choice while remaining happily interdependent.
