<details>
<summary>On InnoCentive, what was the relationship between a solver's distance from their expertise and their likelihood of solving a challenge?</summary>

The further a problem was from a solver's own area of expertise, the more likely they were to solve it. A lawyer solved a molecular synthesis challenge by thinking about tear gas. A retired Sprint engineer solved NASA's thirty-year solar particle storm problem using radio wave techniques. Outsiders bring knowledge from unexpected domains that insiders cannot see -- their lack of domain expertise is a feature, not a bug.
