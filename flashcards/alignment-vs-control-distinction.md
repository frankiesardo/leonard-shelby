<summary>What is the difference between AI alignment and AI control, and why does Tegmark say most people who want "safe AI" are actually only asking for alignment?</summary>

**Control** = you can shut it down. The machine is subject to your authority.
**Alignment** = the AI pursues goals compatible with human values, but it may be smarter than you and you may not be able to stop it.

Most AI safety work focuses on alignment — building AI that *wants* good things. But an aligned superintelligence that you can't control is still the boss of Earth; it just happens to be a benevolent one. Tegmark says this implies the voluntary overthrow of human government, which politicians would find unacceptable if they understood it. The two goals need to be held simultaneously, but they are usually conflated.
