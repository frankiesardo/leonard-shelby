<details>
<summary>Writing feels hard and slow today. What's wrong?</summary>

Nothing. The difficulty is the work, not a sign that something has gone wrong. "Flow" is something the reader experiences, not the writer. The myth that good work should pour out effortlessly makes people interpret normal difficulty as personal failure, which leads to avoidance, "writer's block," and eventually quitting. Your labor is a sign of engagement, not defeat.

See: [Difficulty as Engagement](../ideas/difficulty-as-engagement.md)
