<summary>A manager talks about cooperation but runs an internal contest where one person wins a trip. What's the underlying paradigm conflict?</summary>

The manager has a Scarcity Mentality dressed in Abundance rhetoric. The reward system is Win-Lose (one winner, everyone else loses), but the words say Win-Win. You can't get the fruits of cooperation from the roots of competition.

The Abundance Mentality says there's enough success for everyone; the Scarcity Mentality sees life as a finite pie. Without switching the underlying paradigm — and the systems that express it — the cooperation talk is empty.

See: [Abundance Mentality](../ideas/abundance-mentality.md)
