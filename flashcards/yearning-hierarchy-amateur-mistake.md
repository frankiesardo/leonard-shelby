<summary>What is the "amateur mistake" in building a yearning hierarchy, and why does it backfire?</summary>

The amateur mistake is putting too many yearnings on the top shelf and too few on the bottom shelf. Since your time and energy are severely limited, prioritization is zero-sum: the more things you put on the top shelf, the less likely any of them are to thrive. The fewer yearnings on top, the better their chances. Deprioritizing is just as important as prioritizing — and most of history's legends had an extremely sparse Non-Negotiable bowl with just one or two things in it.
