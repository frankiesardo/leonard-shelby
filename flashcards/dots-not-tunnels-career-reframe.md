<summary>Why does viewing careers as "dots" instead of "tunnels" reduce the paralysis of career decisions?</summary>

The tunnel metaphor makes every choice feel like a 40-year commitment, which causes tyranny of choice ("I must pick the right one"), fear of switching ("changing means failure"), and identity fusion ("I *am* my job"). The dots metaphor reframes each choice as a short experiment — a hypothesis test with low stakes. You can't connect dots looking forward anyway, so dot #1 is just a first date. The median young person stays in a job only 3 years — careers are already dots whether you frame them that way or not.
