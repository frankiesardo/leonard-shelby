<summary>When Jobs returned to Apple in 1997, the company was making dozens of products and was 90 days from bankruptcy. What was his first strategic move, and what principle did it embody?</summary>

He drew a two-by-two grid: consumer vs. professional on one axis, desktop vs. portable on the other. Apple would make four great products -- one for each quadrant -- and kill everything else. The principle: "People think focus means saying yes to the thing you've got to focus on. But that's not what it means at all. It means saying no to the hundred other good ideas." Focus is not about what you do; it is about what you refuse to do. This applied at every level: product lines, features within products, buttons on devices, options in interfaces.
