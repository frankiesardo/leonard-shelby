<summary>What is "microhumor" and why does Alexander call it the number one thing separating enjoyable writers from technically proficient ones?</summary>

Microhumor is not jokes or comedy — it's the tiniest ghost of a smile on the reader's face. A slightly unexpected word choice, a dry aside, a self-aware parenthetical. The reader may not consciously register it, but their experience is fundamentally different. The behaviorist model: reading provides few reward sources (insights, tribal validation, humor). Without microhumor, the reward signal flatlines between big insights and the reader drifts off. It also disarms — it's hard to hate someone making you laugh, which buys extra goodwill when challenging cherished beliefs.
