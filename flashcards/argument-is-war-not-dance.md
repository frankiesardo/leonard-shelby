**Q:** What is Lakoff and Johnson's foundational claim about the metaphor ARGUMENT IS WAR, and what would change if we replaced it with ARGUMENT IS DANCE?

**A:** The claim is that ARGUMENT IS WAR is not a figure of speech but the actual conceptual structure through which we understand, experience, and conduct arguments. We *attack* positions, *defend* claims, *win* and *lose*, find positions *indefensible*. The linguistic expressions follow from the conceptual mapping. If a culture structured arguments as dance instead of war, participants would be performers aiming for balance and aesthetic harmony, not combatants aiming for victory. They would experience arguments differently, conduct them differently, and talk about them differently. We probably wouldn't even recognize what they do as "arguing." The metaphor doesn't decorate the activity -- it constitutes it.
