<summary>A manager avoids telling a direct report that their presentations are confusing because she doesn't want to hurt their feelings. She genuinely cares about them. Which quadrant of Radical Candor is this, and why is it more damaging than it appears?</summary>

This is Ruinous Empathy -- caring personally without challenging directly. It's the most common management failure because it comes from good instincts (empathy, kindness). But the damage compounds: the employee never improves, colleagues grow resentful, and when the problem finally becomes undeniable, the correction is far more painful than an early honest conversation would have been. Kim Scott's employee Bob was fired after ten months of Ruinous Empathy -- and his response was "Why didn't you tell me?" The "nice" thing was actually the cruelest thing.
