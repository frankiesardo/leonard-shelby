<summary>A colleague complains constantly about their boss but never takes action. Which concept explains the dynamic, and what are the three problem types?</summary>

They're focused on their Circle of Concern instead of their Circle of Influence. Covey identifies three problem types:

1. **Direct control** (your own behavior) — solve by working on habits
2. **Indirect control** (others' behavior) — solve by changing your methods of influence
3. **No control** (things you can't change) — solve by changing your attitude

The colleague is treating an indirect-control problem as a no-control problem. They could have a candid conversation, propose alternatives, or change their response — but instead they're stuck in the Circle of Concern, which shrinks their influence further.

See: [Circle of Influence](../ideas/circle-of-influence.md)
