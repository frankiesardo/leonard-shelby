<summary>What are Matthew Dicks's five tools for sustaining audience attention (the stakes toolkit)?</summary>

1. **Elephant** — plant something big early to create wonder
2. **Backpack** — reveal your plan so the audience carries your hopes
3. **Breadcrumbs** — drop hints without revealing the full picture
4. **Hourglass** — slow time down at peak tension with sensory detail
5. **Crystal ball** — predict a terrible future to create worry

The key: don't front-load stakes. Build through the first half, then let the second half be the roller coaster.
