<summary>Why is Naval's claim that "happiness is a skill" uncomfortable rather than empowering?</summary>

Because if happiness is a skill, then unhappiness is at least partly a training failure — not a circumstance failure. You can't fully blame your genes, your childhood, your boss, or your bank account. Naval's challenge: "If you're so smart, how come you aren't happy?" This reframes the problem from "I need better conditions" to "I need better practice." It also means there's no excuse to wait — you can start training now, just like you'd start training your body.

See: [Happiness as Skill](../ideas/happiness-as-skill.md), [The Backwards Law](../ideas/the-backwards-law.md)
