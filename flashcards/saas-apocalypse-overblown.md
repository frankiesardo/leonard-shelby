<summary>Why is the "SaaS apocalypse" thesis probably wrong, and what's the better frame?</summary>

IT spend is only 8–12% of enterprise spend. Even if AI could instantly rebuild all software, companies would save at most 12%. The "innovation bazooka" should be pointed at the other 90% — core competitive advantage, process optimization — not at rebuilding payroll.

Evidence that incumbents are fine: 75% of public SaaS companies raised prices since ChatGPT launched. Price is a measure of product-market fit — companies under serious competitive threat cut prices, not raise them.

The better frame: coding agents will reduce switching costs (the "hostages not customers" problem) and new AI-native categories will emerge that startups own. Secular losers exist, but the blanket narrative is wrong.
