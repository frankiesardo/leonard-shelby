<summary>Why do habits alone lead to a decline in performance over time?</summary>

Once a skill becomes automatic, you stop paying conscious attention to it. You become less sensitive to feedback and stop noticing small errors. Mindless repetition reinforces current habits rather than improving them. The formula for mastery is **Habits + Deliberate Practice**: automate what you can through repetition, then pour your conscious attention into the next frontier. Pat Riley's Career Best Effort program with the Lakers worked because it combined habitual excellence with nightly performance tracking and a demand for 1% seasonal improvement. Without reflection and review, you're just reinforcing the status quo.
