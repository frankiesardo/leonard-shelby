<summary>What are the three laws of mastery according to Pink, and why is mastery compared to an asymptote?</summary>

1. **Mastery is a mindset** -- it requires Dweck's incremental theory of intelligence (the belief that ability grows through effort). Entity theorists avoid challenges; incremental theorists seek them.
2. **Mastery is a pain** -- Ericsson's deliberate practice research shows mastery requires roughly a decade of intense, focused, often unpleasant effort. Grit predicts success better than IQ.
3. **Mastery is an asymptote** -- like the mathematical curve that approaches a line but never touches it, you get closer and closer but never arrive. The joy is in the pursuit. Mastery attracts precisely because mastery eludes.
