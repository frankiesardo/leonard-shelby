<summary>What does it mean that creative work is "an act of perpetual self-authorization"?</summary>

No credential, title, or institution gives you permission to do your work — you give it to yourself, every day. "Perpetual" is the key word: it's not a one-time event. Every time you sit down to create, you face the quiet question "Who asked you to do this?" and the answer has to come from you, again. Education typically teaches the opposite — that your perceptions are unauthorized — and the result is learned helplessness disguised as humility.
