<summary>Why does Fadell say that if you can't write down a list of five names -- your first hires -- before starting a company, you probably shouldn't start?</summary>

Your first hires are disproportionately important because they function as "seed crystals." In chemistry, a seed crystal dropped into a supersaturated solution triggers rapid crystallization -- the whole solution organizes around that nucleus. The same happens with hiring: one outstanding person attracts five more, each of those attracts three more, and entire arms of the organization materialize around that first hire.

In the earliest days, you don't have HR or recruiters. Everything depends on your network and your ability to convince people. If you can't name five people who would join you, you don't yet have the professional relationships needed to build a team. And if those five people won't actually sign on, the idea may not be compelling enough -- their refusal is a signal.
