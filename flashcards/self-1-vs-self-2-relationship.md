<summary>In Gallwey's Inner Game framework, what are Self 1 and Self 2, and what is the "prime factor" in determining performance?</summary>

Self 1 is the conscious, verbal ego-mind -- the "teller" that instructs, judges, and tries to control. Self 2 is the body and unconscious mind -- the "doer" that includes the nervous system, muscle memory, and an intelligence sophisticated enough to coordinate billions of cells in a fraction of a second.

The prime factor in performance is the quality of the *relationship* between them. When Self 1 trusts Self 2 and stays quiet, performance is effortless and fluid. When Self 1 micromanages, the result is tension, overthinking, and the familiar experience of "beating yourself."
