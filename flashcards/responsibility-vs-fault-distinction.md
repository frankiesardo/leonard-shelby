<summary>Your friend says "It's not my fault, so it's not my problem." What distinction is Manson making between fault and responsibility, and why does conflating them keep people stuck?</summary>

**Fault** is past tense — who caused the problem. **Responsibility** is present tense — who must deal with it now. They are not the same thing.

Many problems are not your fault but are still your responsibility. Getting hit by a car is the driver's fault, but recovering from the injury is your responsibility. Conflating the two means you refuse to take responsibility for things you didn't cause, which leaves you powerless — waiting for the at-fault party to fix your life.

Manson's rewrite of the Spider-Man quote: "With great responsibility comes great power." Accepting responsibility, even for problems you didn't create, is the first step to solving them.
