**Q:** What is amor fati, and how does Manson use it as the alternative to hope?

**A:** Amor fati ("love of fate") is Nietzsche's formula for greatness: wanting nothing to be different, not forward, not backward, not in all eternity. Not merely bearing what is necessary, but loving it. Manson positions it as the posture beyond hope -- instead of hoping for a better future (which is always transactional and self-defeating), you act without hope by simply being better in each present moment. "Don't hope for a better life. Simply be a better life."
