<summary>Why does "I'm not a smoker" work better than "I'm trying to quit smoking"?</summary>

Because behavior change is most durable when it starts at the identity layer, not the outcome layer. "I'm trying to quit" treats smoking as something you do that needs willpower to stop. "I'm not a smoker" redefines who you are -- the temptation doesn't arise the same way because it conflicts with your self-concept. Every action is a vote for the type of person you wish to become; enough votes and the identity becomes self-reinforcing.

See: [Identity-Based Habits](../ideas/identity-based-habits.md)
