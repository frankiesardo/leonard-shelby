<details>
<summary>You've just written a powerful fact followed by a sentence explaining why it's powerful. What should you do?</summary>

Cut the explanatory sentence. If the fact is strong enough to make the point on its own, adding "This is remarkable because..." patronizes the reader and weakens the impact. The reader plays a major role in communication and must be given room to play it. Strong evidence doesn't need a closing argument.

See: [Trust Your Material](../ideas/trust-your-material.md)

</details>
