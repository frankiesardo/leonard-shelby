<summary>What is a "concept handle" and why does Scott Alexander consider it the most valuable thing a writer can produce?</summary>

A concept handle is a catchy phrase that sums up a complex topic — a portable name that lets people carry an idea around and use it to think with. Alexander argues it's the most valuable output because many people have vague, nebulous ideas they can't quite articulate. Crystallizing those into named, manipulable concepts is a big deal. Without a handle, an insight evaporates after reading. With one ("belief in belief," "Pascal's mugging," "applause lights"), the idea becomes permanent cognitive equipment the reader can use to solve other problems.
