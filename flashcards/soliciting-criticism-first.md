<summary>Scott argues that when building a culture of Radical Candor, you should NOT start by giving criticism. What should you do first, and why does this sequence matter?</summary>

Start by soliciting criticism -- asking for feedback about yourself. Have a go-to question ("Is there anything I could do or stop doing that would make it easier to work with me?"), then count to six in the silence. When you get criticism, reward the candor visibly (thank them, act on it, follow up). This sequence matters for three reasons: (1) it establishes that you know you're fallible, which builds trust; (2) it gives you firsthand experience of how criticism feels, making you more empathetic when you deliver it; (3) it demonstrates that feedback flows in all directions, not just top-down, which prevents the vertical dynamic where the boss judges and the report absorbs. Only after you've shown you can take it should you start dishing it out.
