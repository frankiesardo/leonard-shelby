**Q:** What is "the dramatic question" and when should it be present in a story?

**A:** The dramatic question is *Who is this person?* (or, from the character's perspective, *Who am I?*). It emerges at the ignition point -- the moment unexpected change strikes the right character and they react in an unexpected, revealing way. It should be present in every compelling scene. Where the question is being asked and answered in a new way, the reader is engaged. Where it is absent, they drift. The question is powerful because the protagonist themselves does not know the answer -- they discover who they are moment by moment as the pressure of drama is applied, and are often surprised by who they turn out to be.
