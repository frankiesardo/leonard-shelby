<summary>What is "the jump to universality" and what are its key historical examples?</summary>

The jump to universality is the sudden transition a system makes from being able to represent a **limited range** of things to being able to represent **anything** in its domain. It is always abrupt, always transformative, and always marks the beginning of open-ended progress.

Key examples:
- **Writing:** Pictograms (one symbol per concept) to **alphabets** (a small set of symbols representing any word)
- **Numbers:** Tally marks to **positional notation** (representing any number)
- **Biology:** Early replicators to **the genetic code** (capable of encoding any viable organism)
- **Computation:** Special-purpose machines to **universal computers** (capable of running any program)
- **The human brain:** Animal cognition (parochial instincts) to **universal explanation** (capable of understanding anything understandable)

The pattern: before the jump, progress is slow and incremental. After, it is explosive and unbounded. The critical question for any system is whether it has crossed the threshold. If it has, its potential is infinite.
