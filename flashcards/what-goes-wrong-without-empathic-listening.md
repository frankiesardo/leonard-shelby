<summary>What are the four autobiographical responses that prevent genuine understanding, and why does Covey call prescribing without diagnosing "malpractice"?</summary>

The four autobiographical responses are:

1. **Evaluate** — agree/disagree from your frame
2. **Probe** — ask questions from your frame
3. **Advise** — offer solutions from your experience
4. **Interpret** — explain their motives based on yours

All four are projection — treating the other person's experience as a variant of your own. "Prescription without diagnosis is malpractice" because you wouldn't trust a doctor who prescribed without examining you. Yet in conversation, we prescribe constantly — offering advice before understanding the problem.

The paradox: seeking first to understand gives you far more influence than seeking first to be understood. When you can describe someone's position better than they can, they become genuinely open to yours.
