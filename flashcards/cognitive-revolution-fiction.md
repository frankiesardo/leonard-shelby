<summary>What enabled Sapiens to cooperate in groups far larger than Dunbar's number (~150), when no other human species could?</summary>

Fictive language -- the ability to talk about things that do not exist. Other animals communicate about real things (predators, food sources). Sapiens uniquely can discuss gods, nations, corporations, and human rights -- shared fictions that bind thousands or millions of strangers into cooperative networks. Personal acquaintance can sustain ~150 relationships. Shared myths have no upper limit. This is why Sapiens rule the world while Neanderthals, who may have been individually smarter and stronger, went extinct.
