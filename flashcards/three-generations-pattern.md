<summary>What are the three generations of a disruptive product, and what is the primary goal of each?</summary>

**V1: Make the product.** Ship your prototype to early adopters. The goal is product/market fit -- proving people will buy it. Customer acquisition costs are high, features are missing, and you will lose money. This is expected.

**V2: Fix the product.** You know what's broken. Fix the problems, widen the market to the early majority, and start achieving gross margins (making money per unit sold, though not enough to cover business costs).

**V3: Build the business.** The product is good. Now optimize the business -- negotiate better deals, reduce costs, build sustainable sales channels, and reach net margins (actual profitability).

The pattern is invariant: make it, fix it, build it. The iPod, iPhone, and Nest all followed it. Expecting profitability from V1 is like asking a baby to run a marathon.
