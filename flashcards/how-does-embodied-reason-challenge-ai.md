<summary>If reason is embodied, what does that imply about machines that manipulate symbols without bodies?</summary>

If reason grows from bodily experience — perception, motor activity, spatial orientation — then machines that only manipulate formal symbols cannot genuinely understand meaning the way humans do. They can simulate aspects of reasoning (the parts that happen to be formalizable), but they lack the embodied grounding that gives concepts their meaning.

This is Lakoff's critique of the computational theory of mind and strong AI: the mind is not a program running on the hardware of the brain. The "hardware" (the body, the environment, the way we physically interact with things) is constitutive of thought, not incidental to it. Basic-level categories — the most natural level of categorization — are defined by motor programs, gestalt perception, and mental imagery, none of which exist in a disembodied symbol system.
