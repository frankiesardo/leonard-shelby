<summary>A tennis student hits 8 out of 10 balls on the racket frame. Gallwey asks her to focus on the seams of the ball and "not try to hit it at all." She hits 9 out of 10 dead center. Why did trying harder make things worse?</summary>

"Trying hard" is Self 1 energy -- the conscious ego-mind seizing control, tightening muscles that should be loose, generating instructions that distract from the task. Self 2 (the body's unconscious competence) already knows how to make contact, but Self 1's interference prevents it from operating.

When Joan focused on the seams, Self 1 was fully occupied with observation and had no bandwidth left for interference. Self 2, finally unimpaired, performed beautifully. The key insight: letting it happen is not making it happen. It is not going limp -- it is letting Self 2 use only the muscles necessary, without the conscious mind adding its own unnecessary effort.
