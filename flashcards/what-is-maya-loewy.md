<summary>What is MAYA (Most Advanced Yet Acceptable), what are the two failure modes, and what product is the textbook example?</summary>

Raymond Loewy's principle: the ideal product or design is as novel as people can psychologically tolerate — no more, no less.

**Too novel** → rejection. The threat response kicks in; the product feels alien or demands too much identity commitment. (Google Glass, early Segway.)

**Too familiar** → indifference. No reason to switch; the product owns no memorable mental slot.

The Prius is the textbook example. Toyota designed a visually distinct silhouette — weird enough to be a visible signal, familiar enough to still be obviously a car. The strange shape was a feature: it let drivers broadcast their values. Technically superior hybrid sedans that looked like normal cars failed because they offered no psychological differentiation.
