<summary>In the original iPhone keynote, Steve Jobs spent several minutes criticizing existing smartphones before revealing the iPhone. Why is "starting with the pain" more effective than starting with the solution?</summary>

This technique is called the "virus of doubt." Humans are loss-averse -- we react more strongly to the threat of losing something (or discovering we've been tolerating something bad) than to the promise of gaining something new. By making the audience feel the pain of their current experience, Jobs primed them to want a cure before he even showed the product.

Without the virus of doubt, people have normalized their suffering. They've adapted to terrible phones and don't know they need something better. The technique makes the invisible pain visible, creating an emotional gap that the product then fills. It works in product launches, investor pitches, internal advocacy -- any time you need someone to take action about a problem they've learned to ignore.
