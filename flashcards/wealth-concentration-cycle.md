<summary>The Durants describe economic history as a "heartbeat." What are the two phases, and what are the two ways societies have responded when concentration reaches a critical point?</summary>

The two phases are **systole** (concentration of wealth) and **diastole** (redistribution). Wealth naturally concentrates because practical ability is unevenly distributed. The rate accelerates under democracy, which allows the most economic freedom.

When concentration reaches a breaking point, societies respond with either:

1. **Legislation redistributing wealth** (Solon's Athens, the New Deal) — peaceful and more effective
2. **Revolution distributing poverty** (Rome's civil wars, the French Revolution) — violent and less effective

The Roman Senate's refusal to compromise produced a century of civil war. Solon's reforms saved Athens within a generation.
