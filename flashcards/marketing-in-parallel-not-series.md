<summary>Why does Rory Sutherland say marketing and innovation should run in parallel rather than in series, and what goes wrong when you sequence them?</summary>

Most companies sequence: build the product → then figure out the marketing. Rory argues this is almost always wrong. The three dimensions — psychological (brand/marketing), technological (product), and economic (pricing/business model) — interact. The marketing insight might change what you build. The psychological frame might determine whether the technology gets adopted at all.

Running them in series means you ship a product and then discover the psychological framing problem you could have solved during design. Running them in parallel means the marketing insight can influence the product before it's built.

"Try and use those three things in parallel. What most businesses do is try and do things in series."
