<summary>What do Klinkenborg's "difficulty as engagement" and Waitzkin's "investment in loss" have in common?</summary>

Both reframe difficulty as the mechanism of growth rather than an obstacle to it. Klinkenborg says the hard, slow feeling of writing is the work itself — not a sign of failure. Waitzkin says deliberately putting yourself in losing positions is how learning happens — ego must yield before growth can begin. In both cases, the instinct to avoid discomfort is exactly what keeps people stuck. The discomfort is the signal, not the problem.
