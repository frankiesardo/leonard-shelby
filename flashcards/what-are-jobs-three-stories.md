<summary>What are the three stories in Jobs's Stanford commencement address, and what lesson does each convey?</summary>

1. **Connecting the dots** — you can't connect the dots looking forward, only backward. Follow your curiosity and trust that it will connect later. (His calligraphy class led to the Mac's typography.)
2. **Love and loss** — getting fired from Apple at 30 freed him to begin again as a beginner, leading to NeXT, Pixar, and his eventual return. Don't settle — keep looking for work you love.
3. **Death** — remembering you'll die soon is the best tool for making big choices. It strips away fear, expectations, and pride, leaving only what truly matters.
