# Evergreen notes turn ideas into objects — Steph Ango

A short, sharp extension of Matuschak's evergreen notes concept from the CEO of Obsidian. The key reframe: evergreen notes turn ideas into **objects**. Once an idea is an object, you can manipulate it, combine it, stack it. You don't need to hold everything in your head at the same time. This is the "concept handle" idea from [Scott Alexander](../nonfiction-writing-advice/summary.md) applied to your own private thinking — give every idea a name short enough to use in a sentence.

## Key ideas

- **Ideas as objects.** The central metaphor. Writing an idea down in atomic form externalizes it. Now it's a thing you can pick up, move around, combine with other things.
- **Composable ideas.** Complex ideas are built up from smaller ones. "Creativity is combinatory uniqueness" is built on top of "Everything is a remix."
- **Titles as sentences.** Each note title should be a statement you can drop into a sentence. This forces clarity and memorability.
- **You don't need to agree.** A note captures an idea worth engaging with, not necessarily one you endorse.
- **Notes can be very short.** No minimum length. If the title says it all, that's enough.

## Connections

- Direct extension of [Andy Matuschak's evergreen notes](../evergreen-notes-matuschak/summary.md).

- The "ideas as objects" metaphor connects to the garden's own design: `/ideas/` is literally a directory of idea-objects.
