# Tobi Lütke: 21 Years of Building Shopify

A conversation with David Senra (Founders podcast) where Tobi Lütke lays out his philosophy of company building as engineering -- not in the narrow software sense, but in the sense of designing systems from first principles. The core insight: companies are social technologies, and most are terrible because people cosplay existing templates instead of engineering from axioms. Tobi spent 15 years cosplaying a "serious CEO" before COVID forced him to rederive everything, and the result was Shopify OS -- a desired-state system that models the company as code, removes politics, and makes every organizational decision legible and computable.

## Key ideas

- **Companies as social technology.** A company is a path-dependent solution to social and legal problems that lets you run a counterfactual against the world and means-test it against a market. The institution is only 500 years old and still poorly understood. If you treat it as a technology rather than a fixed form, you can engineer it.

- **Stop cosplaying, start engineering.** After the IPO, Tobi tried to be a "serious public company CEO" -- delegating, trust-falling, running business lines. It almost killed Shopify. COVID forced a rederivation from axioms. He reviewed every project in 16-hour days, cancelled 60%, turned over every executive, and replaced them with founders from acquired companies. The lesson: your intuition, refined over years, is the asset. Cosplaying someone else's management style destroys it.

- **Desired-state systems for organizations.** Tobi built Shopify OS -- a GitHub repo with config files, Python code, and a SAT solver that computes what the company should look like (titles, levels, reporting ratios, headcount) from agreed-upon principles. You hold the model up against reality, and HR reconciles the delta. This removes enormous amounts of politics: when the sales VP asks for 50 more people, the system shows what breaks elsewhere.

- **Rivalry over competition.** Competition leads to mimicry (Xerox strategy). Rivalry inspires you to be your best. Agassi couldn't have been Agassi without Sampras. Michael Jordan made up slights to fuel himself. Copying a seven-out-of-ten solution gives you a seven. Building from scratch gives you a six you have mastery over -- and you can iterate past seven.

- **Differentiation as axiom.** "Make it different, even if it's worse" (James Dyson). If you copy, your ceiling is the original. If you build from tabula rasa, you own every decision and can iterate indefinitely. The SpaceX Raptor evolution photo is "today's Picasso" -- teams moving forward by subtraction, which almost never happens in industry.

- **Environment over policy.** Instead of corporate babyproofing (policies that tell people to override their intuition), design the environment so the right thing is intuitive. Pods that seat five uncomfortably fit seven -- no policy needed for team size. Meeting rooms go in dark interior spaces so people don't spend all day in meetings. Norman doors are a design crime that signals broken taste.

- **Identity-level affirmations work.** The brain is a "retrospective narrative alignment mechanism" -- it reconciles your history with your current self-identity. You can hack this: Tobi wrote "I love public speaking" for a week and it rewired him. He asks executives to commit publicly to what they'll present on stage, knowing their brain will reconcile them toward that commitment. Stoic separation of tasks: control what you can, be worthy of the outcome, and let the rest go.

- **Hiring for spikes and agency, not credentials.** Shopify hires for "high agency experience" -- when something went wrong in your story, what did you do minute by minute? They look for founders, entrepreneurs, people who started Shopify stores. Credentials don't matter (Tobi is a high school dropout). Spikiness over well-roundedness. The talent takes care of itself if you build a company worth working for.

## Key quotes

> "Companies are technologies themselves... a path-dependent solution to social and somewhat legal problems."

> "One of the saddest days of my life was when I opened old code and was really impressed with how good it was."

> "You can make a seven-out-of-ten solution to everything by copying the seven-out-of-ten that are already out there. But if you make from tabula rasa your own version of it, you have mastery over it. Even if it's a six out of ten, you can iterate on this."

> "The consequences of bad decisions in software engineering are an externality that doesn't accrue back to the engineers who caused it in almost all instances."

> "Actively avoiding to write something down is sometimes the most important thing you can possibly do."

> "I found the cheat code to always being right is just change your opinion every time you get better information."

## Connections

- The rivalry-over-competition idea maps directly onto [The Art of Competing](../../../ideas/the-art-of-competing.md) from The Inner Game of Tennis -- both argue that the opponent is a gift, not a threat, and that true competition is self-transcendence.
- Tobi's "cosplaying a CEO" epiphany is a lived example of [Self-Authorization](../../../ideas/self-authorization.md) -- he spent years performing someone else's script before authorizing himself to lead as an engineer.
- The Shopify OS desired-state system is [Systems over Goals](../../../ideas/systems-over-goals.md) applied to org design -- you define the system's constraints and let outcomes emerge, rather than chasing headcount targets.
- "No corporate babyproofing" rhymes with [Protect the New](../../../ideas/protect-the-new.md) inverted: instead of shielding fragile ideas from the org's immune system, you design the environment so the immune system doesn't trigger in the first place.
- The "for example" trick (framing feedback as suggestion, not correction) is a practical application of [The Braintrust](../../../ideas/the-braintrust.md) principle -- remove authority from the feedback to keep both sides honest.
- Tobi's explicit embrace of the Adlerian [Separation of Tasks](../../../ideas/separation-of-tasks.md) and stoic philosophy: "Make yourself worthy of being liked; if someone dislikes you, that's their task."
- His insistence on first-principles rederivation connects to [The Outsider Advantage](../../../ideas/the-outsider-advantage.md) -- staying in Ottawa rather than Silicon Valley gave him the distance to avoid cross-pollination entropy and build something genuinely different.
- The SpaceX Raptor photo as "progress by subtraction" connects to [Taste as Competitive Advantage](../../../ideas/taste-as-competitive-advantage.md) -- knowing what to leave out is the hardest and most valuable design skill.
