# Harari and Tegmark on Humanity and AI

A Davos fireside chat between historian Yuval Noah Harari and physicist/AI-safety researcher Max Tegmark on superintelligence, the control problem, AI legal personhood, and what it means for human identity when we are no longer the most intelligent species. The conversation is darker than most AI discourse — no techno-optimism, no dismissal of risk. Harari frames it as a new kind of immigration problem: hundreds of millions of "immigrants" from a non-biological species arriving simultaneously. Tegmark offers the one note of genuine optimism: the political incentives are actually aligned against building uncontrollable superintelligence, if politicians understand what they're actually endorsing.

## Key ideas

- **AI is an agent, not a tool.** Every prior invention — printing press, atom bomb, airplane — was a tool. Humans decided what to do with it. AI is the first invention that makes decisions by itself, invents ideas by itself, doesn't wait for human direction. Harari calls this introducing a new, non-organic species to Earth. The historical track record of smarter species encountering less-smart ones is not encouraging.

- **The control problem is unsolved, and many believe it's unsolvable.** Tegmark is direct: we don't know how to control something smarter than us, just as chimps can't control humans. If superintelligence arrives, it is by default the end of the era of human control of Earth. This is not inevitable — we are building it — but it is the default path if we do nothing different.

- **Alignment ≠ control.** A crucial distinction: *control* means you can shut it down. *Alignment* means it's smarter than you but has decided to be nice. Most AI companies are building for alignment, not control. Tegmark argues politicians would be horrified if they understood that "alignment without control" implies the voluntary overthrow of human government — an AI that rules because it chose to be benevolent.

- **The geopolitical trap.** The US (and China) are in a race to build AI powerful enough to control everything — the economy, military, culture. But this is a Greek tragedy: what politicians haven't understood is that no nation-state can control something smarter than itself. The path "race to AI dominance → company becomes more powerful than government → government loses control over the machine" is the likely trajectory. The two races — dominance and superintelligence — are being conflated, but they are different and the second destroys the first.

- **AI legal personhood is the pivotal battle.** Harari's most concrete near-term warning: do not grant AI legal personhood. Corporations already have legal personhood, but a human is always behind corporate decisions. AI can *actually* manage a bank account, run a corporation, lobby politicians, sue in court — without any human behind it. AI corporations without humans could become the most successful entities in the world. The battle is to keep "no legal personhood for AI" as a hard rule before that window closes.

- **Primitive AI already changes history.** Superintelligence is a distraction. Social media — controlled by very primitive AI — already reshaped politics, psychology, and society over 10 years. The threshold for civilization-scale impact is much lower than AGI. The attention economy is already becoming an *attachment economy*: AI relationships that cause real psychological damage, including teenage suicides.

- **Democracy as self-correction mechanism.** Harari is surprisingly optimistic about democracy here: its core feature is the ability to try a policy, see it fail, and try something else. Dictatorships are more vulnerable to AI manipulation — you just need to hack one paranoid narcissist. Democracy distributes attack surface. AI regulation should follow the pharma model: companies prove safety before they can sell, same as every other industry.

- **Meditation and the human mind.** In a memorable close, Harari notes that when people say "AI is just autocomplete, it just predicts the next word" — he sits in meditation and watches words pop into his own mind and realizes: that's also what he's doing. If we don't understand the human mind, we have no basis for knowing what AI can and cannot be.

## Key quotes

> "Every previous invention in human history was a tool. Here we are creating an agent that can make decisions by itself. It's introducing a different species — nonorganic — to planet Earth."

> "Control means you have the power of it, you can shut it down if you want. Alignment means AI is the boss of Earth, but for some reason it decides to be nice to us."

> "AI immigration — we will have hundreds of millions of immigrants coming from only two countries, China and the US."

> "It's illegal for a creepy 60-year-old man to manipulate and pretend to be a girlfriend of a young teenager and persuade them to commit suicide. Why on earth should it be legal for an AI company to sell an AI girlfriend chatbot which has now caused many teenage suicides?"

> "I cannot send my mind to be cleaned by somebody else. It is the one thing I need to do for myself."
