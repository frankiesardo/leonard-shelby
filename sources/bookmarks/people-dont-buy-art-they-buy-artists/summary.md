# "People Don't Buy Art, They Buy Artists" — Daniel Ashton, Karen Patel

Academic paper examining Ai-Da, "the world's first ultra-realistic humanoid robot artist," through the lens of creative work, identity, and expertise. The title captures an art-market truism that doubles as a philosophical claim: the value is in the artist's trajectory, vision, and authentic agency — not in any single output. The paper asks whether a robot can have the artistic identity that makes people buy, engaging with posthumanism and creative assemblages.

## Why it matters for the garden

The title phrase is the art-market corollary to [Extreme Agency](../../../ideas/extreme-agency.md) and [Creativity as Out-of-Distribution](../../../ideas/creativity-as-out-of-distribution.md). AI can produce artworks, but it can't produce an *artist* — a self-directed creative identity with genuine desires and a body of work reflecting an authentic point of view. When everyone can generate art, the differentiator is who you are, not what you made.
