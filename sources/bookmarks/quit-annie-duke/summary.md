# Quit — Annie Duke

A former professional poker player's case for strategic quitting. Duke argues that we systematically over-stay in failing endeavors due to sunk cost fallacy, loss aversion, escalation of commitment, and identity attachment. The book provides operational frameworks: kill criteria (precommitted exit conditions), the Fresh Start Test ("would I choose this today?"), and the Close-Call Rule (when it's genuinely a coin flip, quit — because the bias is toward staying).

## Key frameworks (from research, not yet from reading)

- **Kill criteria (States and Dates):** "If by [date] I haven't [reached state], I quit." Set before starting.
- **The Fresh Start Test:** "Knowing everything I know now, would I choose this today?"
- **The Close-Call Rule:** Based on Levitt's coin-flip study — when it's genuinely a coin flip, quit. We systematically over-stay.
- **Separate starters from stoppers:** The people who initiate projects should not be the same people who decide to kill them.
