## The idea

An AI companion app that is not a friend, not a therapist, not an assistant. It's a **thinking practice** — a coach, tutor, challenger, Buddhist master. It watches you engage with ideas and pushes you to think harder. It sees your potential and doesn't let you off the hook.

The reference model is **OpenCode/Claude Code**: not an assistant that does things *for* you, but something that watches you work and helps you reflect. The difference matters. You stay in the driver's seat.

## The yoga reframe (key insight)

The product is not a skill-transfer tool. It's a **practice** — like yoga, like meditation, like lifting.

You don't graduate from your yoga class. You don't outgrow it when you "know the poses." The practice *is* the thing. You pay £15/month for years because the space, the instruction, and the commitment device are worth it. You know the forms; you go because she pushes you and you give it your best in that uninterrupted hour.

This kills the dependency paradox: the question "will people stop needing it?" is the wrong question. You don't ask it about yoga.

Business model: monthly subscription, 3 sessions/week, indefinitely.

## What it actually does

1. **Captures your intellectual life** — bookmarks, readings, half-formed ideas. Native Share on phone → saves URL, extracts content, connects it to your existing idea graph.

2. **Builds a model of your mind** — your positions, tensions, blind spots, intellectual trajectory. The longer you use it, the richer the model. This is the moat.

3. **Shows up proactively** — like a coach texting you. "You saved three things about agency this week. Want to dig in?" It tracks the constraint: is it knowledge? Clarity on what you want? Courage?

4. **Micro-interactions, not sessions** — no scheduled sit-down. Two modes:
   - *You initiate*: share a URL → agent processes it → fires back one sharp provocation → you reply when you feel like it → back and forth until done
   - *Agent initiates*: push notification (PWA) → "you saved three things about agency this week, here's a tension" → you reply on the bus → a few exchanges → done
   The depth comes from hundreds of these over months, not from structured sessions.

5. **Tracks trajectory** — over time it can show you where your thinking has improved and where the next edge is. Thinking quality as something you can actually see develop.

## The X-shaped hole connection

The best session goal isn't "make you smarter" (vague). It's **help you discover and articulate the X-shaped holes that only you can fill** — your specific desires, strengths, fears, what moves you and why. Generic AI can't do this because it doesn't have your history. This one does.

## Positioning

**Not** Zettelkasten / knowledge management / second brain. That audience has a system and wants tools that fit it.

**Target:** Readwise users and heavy Pocket/Instapaper users — people who already save things compulsively, believe in investing in their intellectual life, but feel like content isn't compounding. They're doing the behavior. They just don't have the challenge loop.

Pitch: *"You already save what matters to you. Now something pushes you to actually think about it."*

Pocket was discontinued by Mozilla. Readwise has the spaced repetition but no challenger. That's the gap.

## Open questions

- **What makes a provocation good vs bad?** The agent initiating a notification needs to earn your attention. What's the quality bar? When does it push vs stay quiet?
- **Measurement**: what is "thinking quality"? Needs concrete rubric — identifying hidden assumptions, finding the crux, updating priors on evidence. Without this the coaching loop breaks.
- **Cold start**: the garden worked because of accumulated context. Day-one experience for new users needs thought. Maybe the bookmarking onboarding is the answer — you start by saving, the coach works with what you have.

## Platform / form factor

- **Phone**: PWA with Native Share as entry point. Share any URL from Safari/Chrome → triggers save + connect. Chat interface for sessions. No app store friction.
- **Laptop**: Agent running locally (OpenCode server mode, or thin FastAPI wrapper over Claude API) with garden files as context. Cloudflare Tunnel (not ngrok) for fixed public URL.
- **Why local laptop**: residential IP for YouTube transcripts and other bot-blocked sites; full access to garden filesystem; full Claude toolchain.
- **MVP path**: this garden + these skills + a PWA frontend + Cloudflare Tunnel. The product already exists in prototype form. The question is whether other people want it.

## Platform risk response

The a16z thesis: differentiated personas will win, labs converge toward mediocre because of politics, you just need to be 6 months ahead. The moat isn't "AI that remembers you" (everyone will have that). The moat is the **accumulated model of your specific thinking** — your blind spots, your intellectual trajectory, your X-shaped holes. That's not replicable from a standing start.
