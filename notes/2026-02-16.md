# 2026-02-16

"Originality is merely skill in concealing origins." — C. E. M. Joad

Started building the garden today. The irony of beginning a system for original thinking with a quote about the impossibility of originality is not lost on me. But that's the point — the garden isn't about generating ideas from nothing. It's about *connecting* ideas. Every insight is a remix. The skill is in the combination, and in knowing which combinations matter.

Actually there are two distinct skills here: *creating* concept handles (compressing a fuzzy idea into a crisp, reusable phrase) and *remixing* concept handles (combining existing handles into new configurations). The garden is mostly a remixing engine — but the handle-creation step is where the real leverage is. A good handle makes an idea portable. A bad one buries it.

Maybe draw some parallels between the Theory of Constraints in The Goal and The Art of Learning — where he explores games with only a few pieces first?

This is also why startups, lean methodology, and the whole constraint-hunting mindset fascinate me. Lean is essentially a *discipline for finding the constraint* — the bottleneck, the riskiest assumption, the thing that matters most right now. The Goal says identify the constraint and subordinate everything else to it. Lean says the same thing but calls it "validated learning" and "build-measure-learn." Same skeleton, different skin.
