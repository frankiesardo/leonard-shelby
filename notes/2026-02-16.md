# 2026-02-16

"Originality is merely skill in concealing origins." — C. E. M. Joad

Started building the garden today. The irony of beginning a system for original thinking with a quote about the impossibility of originality is not lost on me. But that's the point — the garden isn't about generating ideas from nothing. It's about *connecting* ideas. Every insight is a remix. The skill is in the combination, and in knowing which combinations matter.

First three sources: Scott Alexander on writing, Andy Matuschak on evergreen notes, Steph Ango on ideas-as-objects. All three are about the same thing from different angles — how to externalize thinking so it compounds instead of evaporating. Concept handles, atomic notes, composable ideas. Different names, same fundamental move: take the vague thing in your head and turn it into a solid thing you can pick up and use later.
